# Supporting Character Canon Policy

> Current policy after V0.77 character-canon reconciliation.  
> Supporting characters are documented proportionally to their narrative function.  
> This policy prevents both underwritten caricatures and unnecessary principal-character expansion.

## 1. Core distinction

```text
A supporting character is not an unfinished principal character.
```

Principal characters carry one or more of the following:

- playable route weight;
- a distinct answer to the central couple crisis;
- recurring modular scene pools;
- independent adult progression;
- major relationship-state consequences;
- enough screen time that voice, biography, desire, and route logic require a full source of truth.

Supporting characters exist primarily to:

- make another character's life credible;
- carry history, work, family, friendship, or relationship context;
- make consequences human;
- create constraints, witnesses, obligations, or ordinary texture;
- support a scene without becoming its hidden protagonist.

## 2. Documentation levels

### Level A — principal character

Requires:

- full concrete canon file;
- adult-route integration where relevant;
- voice and progression rules;
- modular scene engines;
- deprecation map when older material can mislead;
- general-document synchronization.

Current Level A characters:

- Marie;
- Sandra;
- Player;
- Mathilde;
- Pauline;
- Nico;
- Raphaëlle.

### Level B — supporting character

Usually documented inside the principal character file whose life they anchor.

Needs enough detail to establish:

- relationship and history;
- occupation or ordinary role when relevant;
- recognizable behavior;
- what they want in the immediate relationship;
- what they know and do not know;
- boundaries and humanity;
- likely reactions to discovery or consequence;
- anti-caricature guardrails.

Does not automatically require:

- independent long arc;
- standalone NSFW engine;
- full wardrobe catalogue;
- complete family tree;
- independent route-state system;
- separate deprecation map;
- equal documentation volume to a principal character.

### Level C — recurring minor character

May need only:

- name;
- concrete function;
- one or two recognizable traits;
- relationship to the relevant principal character;
- continuity facts required by scenes.

### Level D — functional extra

Needs only what the scene requires.

Do not create false canon depth that future writers must preserve unless the detail matters.

## 3. When a standalone file becomes justified

Create a standalone support-character file only if at least one material threshold is crossed:

- the character becomes a playable or sustained adult route;
- the character receives an independent multi-scene arc;
- the character regularly interacts with several principal routes;
- their knowledge, secrets, consent, or consequences require independent tracking;
- they become a recurring direct participant in trio, group, NTR/cuckold, or other adult content;
- runtime implementation needs a distinct state model;
- the Product Owner promotes them into the principal ensemble.

Even then, begin with a short focused support file unless the new role truly requires a full principal-character profile.

## 6. Adult-route rule

A supporting character affected by adult content remains human even without a standalone file.

The documentation must still preserve:

- whether they know;
- whether they consent;
- whether they are betrayed;
- whether their image or privacy is affected;
- their plausible immediate reaction;
- consequences for the principal route.

Examples:

```text
Bastien's ignorance is not cuckold consent.
Jeff's absence from the screen is not permission to erase him.
Maud's photography role is not consent on Raphaëlle's behalf.
A supporting character cannot serve as a consent proxy.
```

## 7. Scene-writing rule

When a supporting character appears:

1. Read the principal full-canon file that defines them.
2. Use only the facts required by the current scene.
3. Keep voice and behavior recognizable but proportional.
4. Do not invent a hidden independent route unless validated.
5. Do not make them omniscient.
6. Do not turn them into a villain solely to clean a principal character's adult choice.
7. Record any new continuity fact in the principal file or validated source pack.

## 8. Runtime rule

Supporting characters do not automatically receive:

- affection variables;
- route flags;
- dedicated inbox threads;
- scene pools;
- proof systems;
- independent endings.

Add runtime state only when a validated modular scene or consequence requires it.

```text
Documentation weight and runtime weight
must follow narrative function,
not the mere existence of a named character.
```

## 9. Promotion rule

If a support character materially changes role:

1. document the Product Owner decision;
2. define the new narrative function;
3. decide whether the character becomes principal or remains enhanced support;
4. update the relevant principal canon;
5. create a focused standalone file only if needed;
6. update reading order, index, narrative status, NSFW canon, and implementation guidance;
7. avoid retroactively treating old support detail as a complete route foundation.

## 10. Final rule

```text
Supporting characters need enough truth to create consequence.
They do not need equal narrative mass.

Develop them when the story asks more of them,
not because the documentation set looks asymmetrical.
```
