## 4. Current consequence principles

- consequence is not automatic punishment;
- debt is not a score or currency;
- proof is not complete truth;
- confession is not automatic repair;
- deletion does not erase knowledge or copies;
- refusal is not automatically betrayal;
- privacy is not automatically a secret;
- participants' mutual consent does not create the consent of an excluded partner;
- due consequences and aftermath outrank new high-intensity opportunities;
- a state that cannot change a future scene becomes historical rather than permanently active.

- the first release is a complete but extensible season;
- scenes serve routes and do not create them;
- sequences are designed before scenes and days;
- every complete playable day offers at least three distinct female-focused visual contents;
- every major consequence has a source fact;
- every active obligation identifies who owes what to whom;
- every secret identifies excluded people;
- consequences and aftermath outrank new opportunities when due;
- multiple hidden routes create collision pressure rather than unlimited accumulation;
- no character's consent is granted by a partner, friend, image, costume, jealousy, or route score;
- Nico remains heterosexual;
