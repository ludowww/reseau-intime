## 2. Global character rules

### Characters must be readable in private messages

A character should be recognizable by:

- rhythm;
- avoidance pattern;
- concrete daily material;
- way of sending or refusing traces/photos;
- way of becoming vulnerable;
- way of making Player act or fail to act.

### Desire is not enough

No character should exist only as a route or sexual possibility.

Each scene needs at least one concrete non-route anchor:

- work;
- domestic life;
- food;
- fatigue;
- social plan;
- family trace;
- object;
- timing;
- ordinary annoyance;
- small habit.

### Player must remain active but imperfect

Player is not a blank avatar and not a perfect seducer.

He can:

- answer badly;
- answer late;
- joke to hide discomfort;
- notice a detail;
- miss the simple human move;
- make one precise sentence, then retreat;
- be more honest than expected, but not always.

## 3. Marie — current canon

### Core identity

Marie is the living center of the couple.

She is not only:

- the woman Player may lose;
- the jealous partner;
- the person asking him to put the phone down.

She is:

- lively;
- social;
- funny;
- tender;
- active;
- sometimes piquant;
- capable of naming a limit;
- capable of moving instead of waiting.

### Concrete life

- Age: around 31.
- Relationship: five years with Player, three years living together.
- Not married, no children.
- Work: communications and small-events coordinator at `La Verrière`, a local cultural / third-place venue.
- Work material: posters, newsletters, invitations, room setup, artists / associations, city contacts, providers, bad logistics, Élodie as work-color witness.

### Domestic anchors

Use often, lightly:

- tomatoes;
- bread;
- burrata / cheese;
- groceries;
- couch;
- half-watched series;
- walks after dinner;
- entrance mirror;
- shoes near the door;
- overfull bag;
- laundry;
- small apartment disorder;
- plants badly kept.

### Personal anchors

- Black dress.
- Gold earrings.
- Lipstick.
- Flower-orange / warm-skin perfume note if needed.
- Playlist while getting ready.
- Mirror / movement / going out.

### Wound

Marie fears being loved as a habit.

Reference:

```text
Je ne veux pas être aimée comme une habitude.
```

Do not overuse this exact line. Use the principle.

### Repair / reaction mode

Marie does not disappear quietly like Sandra.

She moves:

- puts music on;
- gets ready;
- changes clothes;
- goes out;
- calls Pauline;
- walks;
- becomes visible elsewhere;
- asks for a concrete act instead of another explanation.

### Writing guardrails

Avoid reducing Marie to:

- `pose ton téléphone`;
- `regarde-moi`;
- suspicion too early;
- a purely moral obstacle;
- a woman waiting for Player to choose.

Prefer:

- a small shared action;
- a domestic test of presence;
- a social movement;
- a concrete invitation;
- a precise but not melodramatic limit.

## 4. Sandra — current canon

### Core identity

Sandra is a woman of concrete traces.

She does not confess first. She sends, keeps, removes, or comments on a trace.

She is:

- soft;
- prudent;
- complicit;
- emotionally underfed but not available by default;
- reasonable in appearance;
- more alive when Player reads a detail correctly;
- careful with boundaries.

### Concrete life

- In a long stable relationship with Jeff.
- Jeff is around 35, mason, tired, concrete, not a monster.
- Relationship: six years, shared house for around three years.
- Their couple is stable but emotionally underfed.
- Conflicts often end with Sandra withdrawing rather than exploding.

### Work anchor

Sandra works at SentryCore, a software incident / support platform.

She handles first-line incidents and user reports:

- strange tickets;
- missing buttons;
- status bugs;
- repeated notifications;
- clients saying urgent things imprecisely;
- handovers;
- tired end-of-day notes.

### Horaires décalés clarification

Do not write `shift` as the main term.

Use French, concrete phrasing:

- `poste du matin`;
- `poste du soir`;
- `poste de nuit`;
- `horaires décalés`;
- `week-end travaillé`;
- `fin de poste`.

Meaning:

```text
Sandra sometimes works at unusual hours.
This creates tired evenings, late replies, early mornings, and intimate gaps in ordinary social time.
```

This is not a major plot device. Use it only when it gives a natural reason for fatigue, late messages, or a trace after work.

### Refuge anchors

Sandra recovers through:

- couch;
- plaid;
- hot chocolate;
- bedroom door closed;
- paper book;
- scrapbooking;
- printed photos;
- decoration / frames;
- parents / family rhythms;
- countryside house.

### Maison des Tilleuls

Family countryside house.

Use as later anchor, not necessarily J1:

- old gate;
- family photo ritual;
- outdoor table;
- village rhythm;
- childhood room;
- path / market / bakery.

### Readings

Sandra reads paper romances and slow-burn impossible-love stories.

Use carefully. This colors her fantasy life but should not become exposition.

Motifs:

- impossible love;
- being chosen;
- delayed confession;
- slow burn;
- controlled fantasy;
- the courage to show because distance makes it possible.

### Voice

Sandra uses:

- `haha` as a door ajar, not a simple laugh;
- small disclaimers;
- soft boundary lines;
- `officiellement` only with moderation;
- possessives such as `mon cher Player` only when earned.

### Scene engine

Preferred Sandra scene engine:

```text
concrete trace -> Player notices something -> Sandra minimizes -> Player is partly right -> Sandra dodges or smiles -> soft boundary
```

### Writing guardrails

Avoid Sandra as:

- vague light / vague nature only;
- always waiting;
- too available;
- direct route activation too early;
- a woman who glides into betrayal because Player insists.

Sandra does not give because Player pushes.

She may choose to show because she feels seen.

## 5. Player — current canon

### Core identity

Player is discreet, observant, and often evasive.

He is not empty, but he should remain playable.

He is:

- around 32-34;
- in a five-year relationship with Marie;
- living with Marie for around three years;
- competent but tired;
- observant, sometimes too much;
- bad at acting at the right moment;
- ironic when embarrassed;
- capable of tenderness but often delayed.

### Work anchor

Player works as a functional project / product operations coordinator in a small software or digital-services company.

Use as texture, not a technical exposition:

- meetings;
- specs;
- vague priorities;
- bug reports;
- client returns;
- dashboards;
- too many browser tabs;
- cold coffee;
- late email;
- tired attention.

### Daily anchors

- forgotten bread;
- couch;
- quiet walks;
- simple cooking;
- books bought faster than read;
- series half-followed;
- bad organization;
- small photos as ordinary traces;
- occasional chess.

### Occasional chess

Chess colors Player; it does not define him.

Use rarely:

```text
Je réfléchis trop pour un type qui finit quand même par jouer le mauvais coup.
```

Meaning:

```text
Player can anticipate patterns and still miss simple human action.
```

### Relationship with Marie

He loves Marie.

The problem is not lack of love at first. It is passive delay:

- he treats stability as less urgent;
- he sees late;
- he jokes instead of joining;
- he is physically present but not always emotionally active.

### Relationship with Sandra

He reads Sandra's traces well enough to be dangerous.

But he must not become a perfect reader.

He can:

- notice a smile;
- misread a silence;
- answer too neatly;
- be useful by accident;
- pull back after saying something true.

### Writing guardrails

Avoid Player as:

- empty choice vehicle;
- perfect writer;
- always witty;
- seduction machine;
- noble martyr;
- purely guilty man.

If he sounds too good, flatten one reply.
