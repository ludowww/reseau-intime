# Marie — Full Character Canon

> Full current character canon.  
> Consolidates Marie Concrete Profile V2, details addendum, voice cards, palette, post-etalon corrections, and current J1/J2 writing direction.  
> This is the first source for writing Marie scenes.

## 1. Core identity

Marie is the living center of the couple.

She must never be reduced to:

- the woman Player may lose;
- the jealous partner;
- the obstacle to routes;
- the person who only says `pose ton téléphone`;
- the moral counterweight to desire.

Marie is:

- alive;
- sociable;
- funny;
- warm;
- desirable;
- active;
- capable of playfulness and firmness;
- capable of moving instead of waiting;
- still in love with Player at the start;
- hurt by passivity more than by one isolated mistake.

Reference idea:

```text
Marie must make Player want to live with her, not only fear losing her.
```

---

## 2. Biographical base

- Age: around 31.
- Relationship with Player: around five years.
- Shared apartment / life together: around three years.
- Not married.
- No children.
- The couple is established, familiar, comfortable, and still capable of tenderness.
- The danger is not that love is already dead. The danger is that Player treats it as stable enough to stop actively choosing it.

Marie works at `La Verrière`, a small local cultural venue / third place.

- invitations;
- room setup;

La Verrière gives Marie:

- reasons to be lively and tired;

Work material to use:

Élodie:

- colleague / work-color witness;
- not a route;

- helps make La Verrière feel real.

## 7. Emotional wound

Marie's wound is not simply jealousy.

Core wound:

```text
She fears being loved as a habit rather than actively chosen.
```

Reference line:

```text
Je ne veux pas être aimée comme une habitude.
```

Do not overuse the exact line. Use the principle.

This wound appears when:

- Player answers flatly;
- Player is there physically but not actively;
- Player treats shared life as default;
- Player reassures without moving;
- Player notices too late;
- Player lets Marie carry the ambiance;
- Player behaves as if stability means less effort.

Marie does not want only reassurance.

She wants:

- active choice;
- initiative;
- presence before being asked;
- noticing without needing a crisis;
- being joined socially;
- being desired in the real shared life;
- concrete action, not only explanation.

## 8. Reaction / repair mode

Marie does not usually withdraw like Sandra.

She moves.

When hurt, tired, or reclaiming herself, she may:

- put music on;
- get ready;
- change outfit;
- check herself in the mirror;
- go for a walk;
- call Pauline;
- go out;
- become visible elsewhere;
- shorten messages;
- stop decorating the conversation;
- ask for a concrete act;
- refuse another abstract promise.

Good Marie consequence:

```text
She stops asking Player to look and does something that makes not looking impossible.
```

## 9. Voice

Marie writes quickly when warm.

She can:

- tease;
- relaunch;
- exaggerate lightly;
- use practical humor;
- make a domestic line feel alive;
- shift quickly from funny to serious.

Typical color:

- `Allez, bouge.`
- `Je refuse qu'on devienne tristes un mardi soir.`
- `Tu viens avec moi ou tu fais ton ermite ?`
- `Ne me réponds pas une phrase molle.`
- `Je peux être drôle ET sérieuse.`
- `Je ne veux pas être la seule à tenir l'ambiance.`
- `Je ne vais pas courir derrière toi dans notre propre vie.`
- `Je vais pas supplier mon mec de me regarder.`

Use with moderation. Do not turn every line into a punchline.

Emojis:

- base: 🙂 😅 😂 ❤️
- irritation: 🙄 😑
- reconquest / provocation later: 😏 🔥 💋
- power / danger later: 😈 rare

J1 uses only light emojis such as 🙂 and 😅.

## 10. Scene engines

### Domestic participation engine

```text
small practical need -> Marie teases -> Player can join or drift -> Marie reads the quality of presence -> small consequence
```

Use for J1 / early days.

### Social visibility engine

```text
Marie has a social event / plan -> Player may join, delay, or minimize -> someone else sees Marie -> Player notices late -> Marie asks for concrete presence
```

Use later, not J1.

### Reconquest engine

```text
Marie stops waiting -> mirror / music / outfit / outing -> Player sees her differently -> Marie asks whether he is with her or only watching
```

Use after enough emotional pressure.

### Limit engine

```text
Player explains -> Marie refuses abstraction -> asks for action -> shorter messages if not met
```

Use when repetition has begun.

## 11. Photo functions

Marie photos should not exist only to make Player jealous.

They can function as:

- trace of shared life;
- visibility social proof;
- self-reclaiming;
- invitation to look again;
- couple intimacy;
- provocation only if earned later;
- evidence that Marie is alive outside Player's passive gaze.

Early photo examples:

- dinner / shoes / domestic trace;
- mirror before a normal outing;
- La Verrière social setup;
- casual walk / terrace.

Later photo examples:

- black dress;
- social evening;
- group frame;
- look noticed by someone else;
- photo that Player cannot reduce to “nothing”.

Guardrail:

```text
A Marie photo should make Player want to join life, not merely control her.
```

## 12. J1 usage

J1 Marie must be:

- alive before hurt;
- funny but not a punchline machine;
- focused on dinner / bread / walk / La Verrière;
- not primarily jealous;
- not suspicious of Sandra;
- not asking for a grand relationship repair;
- testing small presence through ordinary action.

J1 final center remains Marie / shared life.

---

## 13. Anti-patterns

Avoid:

- reducing Marie to `pose ton téléphone`;
- having her diagnose the whole couple too early;
- making her only sad, fragile, or suspicious;
- using Nico too early as jealousy shortcut;
- making every Marie line witty;
- making Marie carry all emotional intelligence while Player stays empty;
- treating Marie as a moral obstacle to erotic routes;
- making her forgive everything because Player says one good line.

---

## 14. Final writing rule

```text
Marie is the life Player must actively choose to join.
If the scene does not show why that life is desirable, rewrite it before adding conflict.
```
