## 2. Corrected identity

Current rule:

```text
Sandra does not dislike photographs as a category.
She dislikes losing control over a representation
that someone else can keep, select, show, or interpret.
```

She is capable of:

- taking competent selfies;
- choosing flattering angles;
- making several attempts;
- selecting one image;
- sending a visually confident private image;
- becoming more daring when she controls audience and permanence;
- feeling uncomfortable in public photographs despite knowing she can be attractive.

Her route progresses through shared control, not surrendered control.

## 3. Terms retained with corrected meaning

### `trace imparfaite`

Retained only for a specific historical or contextual image.

It no longer means:

- Sandra prefers blur;
- Sandra is represented mainly through objects;
- Sandra cannot take selfies;
- visual vagueness is her route identity.

It means:

```text
an image Sandra reviewed and chose to keep
because the moment mattered,
even though the framing did not fully represent her
```

### `trace concrète`

Retained.

A trace can be:

- a selected selfie;
- a printed photograph;
- an image she reviewed;
- a frame she moved;
- a deleted image;
- an agreed photograph taken by Player;
- an image kept because of its history.

Objects support the trace.

They must not indefinitely replace Sandra as the visual subject.

### `distance choisie`

Retained.

Distance gives Sandra:

- time to make attempts;
- control over the angle;
- control over sending;
- a clear audience;
- a way to become visually daring without public exposure.

Distance is not visual vagueness.

### `image choisie`

Strengthened.

It means Sandra controls:

- creation or review;
- selection;
- crop;
- audience;
- saving rule;
- whether another image follows.

## 4. Distinction from Pauline

Do not merge the two image engines.

```text
Pauline
= creates several valid versions for several audiences
= controls compartmentalization
= double address

Sandra
= seeks one representation she recognizes
= chooses one trusted audience
= tests whether control can be shared safely
```

A Sandra scene should not default to:

- public version versus secret crop;
- strategic simultaneous audiences;
- reciprocal proof;
- operational compartment management.

Those remain primarily Pauline engines.

A Sandra scene may involve a compromised audience, but the emotional consequence is loss of entrusted representation rather than failure of a compartment strategy.

---

## 5. Distinction from Mathilde

```text
Mathilde
= ordinary sensuality becomes a deliberately chosen effect
= domestic taboo
= I know what this does to you

Sandra
= controlled self-representation becomes entrusted access
= private safety and recognition
= I know what I am allowing you to keep of me
```

Sandra may send a provocative selfie.

The point is not simply that she chose to provoke.

The point is that she selected a representation and trusted Player with its future.

---

## 6. Distinction from Raphaëlle

```text
Raphaëlle
= chosen version, craft, role, transformation, aftermath

Sandra
= chosen representation continuous with her felt self
```

Sandra does not need a mask, persona, fictional role, or transformation to become visually confident.

Her image should feel like a version she recognizes as herself.

---

## 7. Distinction from Marie

```text
Marie
= social visibility, movement, being actively chosen in public and private

Sandra
= public self-consciousness, private authorship, trusted audience
```

Sandra can be more explicit in a private selfie than in a public group photograph.

Marie can become more energized through public visibility.

Do not write the same social-photo progression for both.

## 8. Sequence compatibility map

### S03 — Le ticket fantôme et la photo du déjeuner

Current interpretation:

- the photograph comes from the last lunch;
- it was taken with Sandra's agreement;
- Sandra reviewed and retained it;
- it was not publicly posted;
- she chooses or crops the version sent to Player;
- the important fact is that she kept and selected it, not that it is blurred.

### S09 — La photo imprimée après le poste

Current interpretation:

- Sandra chooses the file before printing;
- printing is an act of validation and permanence;
- the image may represent Sandra directly;
- the print is not necessarily an object-only trace;
- moving or hiding it changes the relationship to the chosen representation.

### S16 — Le café enfin tenu

Current interpretation:

- no photograph is mandatory;
- if one exists, Sandra must know who takes it and what becomes of it;
- the physical meeting may be more intimate precisely because no image is produced.

### S19 — La photographie que Sandra a vraiment choisie

Current interpretation:

- usually a selfie or a photograph Sandra directed and reviewed;
- Player is the intended audience;
- the progression comes from selection, audience, and permanence;
- the image may be visually confident rather than hesitant or vague;
- saving is a separate permission.

### S30 — Sandra choisit ce qu'elle garde

Current interpretation:

- Sandra may keep, print, move, delete, withdraw, or revoke access;
- the decision concerns both emotional meaning and control of representation;
- removal from the gallery or phone does not erase memory or consequence.

### S35 — La dernière photo change de sens

Current interpretation:

- an old Sandra image may return as a chosen secret, restored trust, revoked access, or evidence of betrayal;
- its meaning changes through the history of audience and control.

## 9. Scene-rule compatibility map

When applying `08_REGLES_DES_SCENES_MODULAIRES.md` to Sandra, every visual scene must answer:

```text
Who controlled the camera?
Did Sandra review the image?
Who selected the final frame?
Who is the intended audience?
Can Player only view, or also keep?
Can the image be forwarded?
What happens if Sandra changes her mind?
```

Sandra-specific scene tests become:

- Is there a concrete, precisely sourced trace or self-representation?
- Does Sandra choose, review, or direct the image?
- Is her public discomfort distinguished from her private selfie competence?
- Is viewing distinguished from saving?
- Is saving distinguished from forwarding?
- Does Player respect the representation rather than only compliment the body?
- Does Jeff remain real where relevant?
- Does refusal create a mutation instead of pressure?

## 10. J1 compatibility

The existing soft lunch trace remains compatible only with this origin:

```text
Sandra agreed to the picture.
It existed on her phone.
She reviewed it.
She kept it because the lunch mattered.
She chose the version she sends now.
```

The J1 image must not imply:

- covert capture;
- a random image obtained without her knowledge;
- that blur is her preferred aesthetic;
- that Player may retain future images by default.

The line-level dramatic object is:

```text
why Sandra selected and resent the image
```

not:

```text
why the image is technically imperfect
```

## 11. Deprecated interpretations

The following are deprecated:

- Sandra is afraid of cameras in all contexts;
- Sandra cannot make visually confident selfies;
- Sandra's route should remain object-focused to preserve subtlety;
- blur equals emotional depth;
- every Sandra photo should be soft, dim, distant, or partially hidden;
- Player gains access because he notices a body detail;
- an image sent to Player may automatically be saved;
- her route progresses when she relinquishes control;
- private image management makes her a softer Pauline;
- chosen provocation makes her a slower Mathilde;
- selected version makes her a non-cosplay Raphaëlle.

## 12. Future audit impact

When older source packs and runtime scenes are audited, flag Sandra content for `ADAPTABLE` or `REWRITE` when it contains:

- unclear camera origin;
- image received without Sandra's knowledge;
- object-only visual replacing her repeatedly;
- public discomfort interpreted as inability to create selfies;
- saving rights not stated;

- generic mirror selfie identical to other characters;

## 13. Final rule

```text
The Sandra route is not about making an image clearer.

It is about making ownership, selection, audience, and trust clearer.

Player's privilege is not that Sandra finally lets herself be photographed.
It is that she chooses a representation,
lets him receive it,
and may eventually trust him with part of what happens next.
```
