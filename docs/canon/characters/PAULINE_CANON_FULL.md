# Pauline — Full Character Canon

> Full current character canon from available validated calibration material.  
> Pauline has not yet received the same concrete-profile pass as Marie / Sandra / Player, but this file consolidates all currently validated usable detail.  
> Future Pauline-specific profile work should update this file directly.

## 1. Core identity

Pauline is Marie's loyal friend with a controlled public image and a more dangerous private contradiction.

She is:

- publicly reliable;
- loyal to Marie;
- socially capable;
- funny;
- sharp;
- false-sage;
- dry in private;
- fast in complicity;
- able to test Player;
- not cynical;
- not omniscient;
- not only a woman with photos.

Signature reference:

```text
Je suis sage. En vitrine.
```

---

## 2. Public image

Pauline appears reliable and almost irreproachable.

Public colors:

- Marie's trusted friend;
- social organizer;
- associative / charitable involvement;
- parish / choir / community background;
- family-friendly surface;
- someone people trust;
- someone who looks like she knows what is appropriate.

This public image should not become a caricature.

The parish / church / choir material is a contrast and a social mask, not a joke repeated every scene.

---

## 3. Private contradiction

In private, Pauline can reveal:

- curiosity;
- provocation;
- controlled risk;
- desire to be seen differently;
- attraction to Player;
- shame after a line goes too far;
- need to regain moral surface;
- enjoyment of being clever;
- real loyalty conflict with Marie.

She should not be purely manipulative.

She alternates between:

- shame;
- provocation;
- alibi;
- desire;
- guilt;
- loyalty;
- control;
- wanting to do it again.

---

## 4. Relationship with Marie

Pauline's loyalty to Marie is real.

That is what makes her private tension dangerous.

She may say or imply:

- Marie trusts her;
- she is speaking as Marie's friend;
- she should not enjoy Player's reply;
- she knows where the line is;
- knowing the line does not mean she stays far from it.

Do not write Pauline as a false friend from the start.

If she crosses a line, it must cost something.

---

## 5. Relationship with Player

With Player, Pauline is quicker, sharper, and more private.

She can:

- test his response speed;
- make him answer more intelligently;
- tease his innocence;
- pretend the situation is official / harmless;
- use Marie's trust as pressure;
- create complicity in three messages;
- realize she is waiting for his reply.

Player should not always win the exchange.

Pauline can make him sharper, but not magically good.

---

## 7. Official / unofficial dosage

Pauline can use official / unofficial framing.

But it must not become automatic.

Dosage:

- `official / unofficial`: maximum once per scene;
- `sage / auréole / moral reset`: one or two light touches maximum;
- church / choir / parish: one appearance, preferably as detour or exit beat;
- Marie pressure: present as background, not every ten lines;
- punchlines: present, not every reply.

Good rhythm:

```text
Pauline : Marie m'a demandé de te l'envoyer.
Pauline : Je précise avant que tu m'accuses de prendre des initiatives.

Player : tu prends des initiatives ?

Pauline : Toujours.
Pauline : Mais là j'ai un témoin.
```

---

## 8. Choir / voice-message guardrail

Pauline may talk about singing, rehearsal, or her voice.

But the game must not promise a playable audio message.

Allowed:

```text
Player : Tu pourrais m'envoyer un vocal.
Pauline : Non.
Pauline : Ma voix est sous protection paroissiale.
```

Forbidden:

- `[vocal sent]`;
- `listen to this`;
- a choice around an absent audio file;
- a scene requiring the player to hear Pauline's voice.

## 10. Photos / proof / control

Pauline often relates to images, proof, framing, and control.

But she must not become simply `the woman with photos`.

Photo functions:

- public social trace;
- controlled selfie;
- proof she frames herself;
- image that tests Player;
- image that creates risk for Pauline too;
- later proof that exposes her, not only others.

Guardrail:

```text
Pauline must eventually be exposed as well as exposing.
```

If she only controls images forever, she becomes too powerful and less human.

## 11. Scene engines

### Fast complicity engine

```text
publicly defensible pretext -> Pauline sharpens it -> Player answers -> she enjoys the reply before admitting it
```

### Moral surface engine

```text
private provocation -> Pauline remembers public goodness / Marie / choir / association -> she reframes with dry humor
```

### Control failure engine

```text
Pauline frames a test -> Player answers unexpectedly -> she smiles / waits / loses a little control -> danger begins
```

### Exposed exposer engine

```text
Pauline has proof or image power -> later a trace points back at her -> she must ask for help or set a limit
```

Use later, not J1.

## 13. Anti-patterns

Avoid:

- making Pauline omniscient;
- making her purely cynical;
- making her only a photo distributor;
- overusing `version officielle / officieuse`;
- overusing church / halo jokes;
- forgetting real loyalty to Marie;
- making her cross lines without cost;
- creating a playable voice-message promise;
- making every reply a punchline;
- letting Player be perfectly sharp every time.
