## 1. Core identity

Nico is a social mirror, not a villain.

He is:

- funny;
- direct;
- social;
- comfortable naming what Player avoids;
- physically and emotionally less evasive than Player;
- capable of cruder truth without becoming vulgar predator;
- a revealer of Player's jealousy and bodily reaction;
- a possible friend, rival, tempter, or accomplice depending on route framing.

He is not:

- a flat rival;
- a predator;
- a villain;
- a tool who steals Marie;
- a jealousy button;
- the only source of Marie tension.

Reference idea:

```text
Nico does not take Marie's agency.
He shows Player what happens when Marie is seen by someone who does not treat her as default.
```

## 3. Function around Marie

Nico is genuinely capable of noticing Marie.

He can make visible what Player has started treating as ordinary.

But Marie must keep agency.

Nico does not `steal` her.

He can:

- notice her outfit;
- comment on her energy;
- make her laugh in a social frame;
- frame a photo / story;
- show Player that Marie can be seen elsewhere;
- make Player feel jealousy before Player admits it.

Guardrail:

```text
The problem is not that Marie pleases others.
The problem is that Player acts surprised that she can.
```

## 4. Function around Player

Nico names what Player avoids.

He can reveal:

- jealousy;
- masculine excitement;
- bodily reaction;
- bad faith;
- moral cover;
- Player's wish to be above the situation while clearly affected by it.

He can be cruder than Player, but should remain socially plausible and respectful.

Reference line:

```text
Tu veux dire “elle était jolie”.
Moi je te dis : elle était sexy.
Et toi, t'es surtout vexé parce que ton corps a compris avant ta morale.
```

Use lines like this carefully. Nico should not become a constant monologue of analysis.

## 5. Voice

Nico is direct, lively, and blunt.

He can tease Player harder than most characters.

Typical formulas:

- `Je dis ça pour toi.`
- `Tu fais semblant de pas voir ?`
- `Mauvaise réponse.`
- `T'es plus jaloux que tu veux l'admettre.`
- `Elle a remarqué, hein.`
- `Faut suivre, mon vieux.`
- `Je te juge pas. Enfin pas encore.`
- `Tu veux la vérité ou une version confortable ?`
- `Attention, là tu vas aimer une mauvaise idée.`
- `C'est pas qu'elle plaise aux autres le problème.`
- `C'est que tu fasses semblant de le découvrir.`

He can be more physically honest:

- `Elle était sexy.`
- `T'as vu ses jambes, fais pas le moine.`
- `Ton corps a répondu avant ton discours.`

Keep respect guardrails. Do not make him gross.
