# Sandra — Full Character Canon

> Full current character canon.  
> Consolidates Sandra Concrete Profile V2, details addendum, final choices addendum, voice cards, color addendum, address formula, and post-etalon corrections.  
> This is the first source for writing Sandra scenes.

## 1. Core identity

Sandra is a woman of concrete traces.

She does not usually confess directly.

She sends, keeps, moves, removes, prints, comments on, or minimizes a trace.

Core formula:

```text
concrete trace -> Player reads something -> Sandra minimizes -> Player is partly right -> Sandra dodges or softens -> boundary
```

Sandra is:

- soft;
- prudent;
- complicit;
- emotionally underfed;
- not available by default;
- reasonable in appearance;
- more alive when seen precisely;
- careful with limits;
- easily embarrassed by feeling too much;
- more likely to retreat than to confront.

She must not be written as:

- vague light only;
- a passive woman waiting forever;
- immediately available;
- melodramatic;
- a photo dispenser;
- someone who gives because Player insists.

Reference principle:

```text
Sandra ne cède pas parce que Player insiste.
Elle choisit de montrer parce qu'elle se sent vue, et parce que la distance lui donne le courage d'oser.
```

## 2. Relationship context

Sandra is in a long-term relationship with Jeff.

- Jeff is around 35.
- He is a mason.
- Sandra and Jeff have been together around six years.
- They have shared a house for around three years.
- Jeff has an evening beer ritual.
- He is concrete, tired, not very verbal.
- He is not a cartoon villain.
- He is not written to excuse everything Sandra feels.

Their couple is stable in appearance but emotionally underfed.

The problem is not permanent war. It is mismatch:

- Jeff wants things said directly;
- Sandra often needs emotional tact;
- Jeff finds her too sensitive;
- Sandra withdraws;
- Jeff reads withdrawal as sulking;
- small hurts accumulate without becoming dramatic scenes.

Possible Jeff phrases, used sparingly and not all at once:

- `Tu fais encore ta tête ?`
- `On va pas en faire un roman.`
- `Tu prends tout trop à cœur.`
- `Ça va, je t'ai rien dit.`
- `T'es encore partie dans ton truc.`
- `Franchement, des fois on dirait que tu cherches à pleurer.`
- `Je suis crevé, Sandra. J'ai pas envie de deviner.`
- `Tu peux pas juste dire les choses normalement ?`
- `Bon, tu reviendras quand ça ira mieux.`

Guardrail:

```text
Jeff can hurt Sandra without being a monster.
That makes Sandra's restraint more credible.
```

Work material:

## 4. Horaires décalés — clarification

Do not use the English word `shift` as a main writing term.

Use French wording:

- `horaires décalés`;
- `poste du matin`;
- `poste du soir`;
- `poste de nuit`;
- `fin de poste`;
- `week-end travaillé`.

Meaning:

```text
Sandra sometimes works outside normal office hours.
This creates fatigue, late replies, early mornings, and moments where she is alone with her phone while the rest of the world is off-rhythm.
```

This is not a gameplay mechanic.

It is not required in every scene.

Use it only when it gives a natural reason for:

- a late message;
- tired softness;
- printing photos after work;
- hot chocolate after a long evening;
- a slightly private hour;
- a delayed reply;
- an early goodnight.

If it clutters a scene, omit it.

## 5. Refuge system

Sandra retreats rather than attacks.

When hurt, embarrassed, tired, or too moved, she may:

- close the bedroom door;
- sit on the couch;
- take a plaid;
- make hot chocolate;
- read a paper book;
- print photos;
- scrapbook;
- move a frame;
- tidy or decorate;
- go silent for a while;
- visit or call her parents;
- return to family rhythms.

House refuge hierarchy:

1. couch + plaid + hot chocolate;
2. bedroom + paper book + closed door;
3. table / scrapbooking / printed photos / frames;
4. family home memory / parents / countryside.

Writing use:

```text
Sandra often does something manual or quiet instead of naming the feeling directly.
```

## 9. Voice

Sandra's voice is restrained, soft, and slightly defensive.

She often uses short to medium messages.

She does not usually machine-gun messages.

When embarrassed, she may split into several small messages.

Typical formulas:

- `Je suis très raisonnable, normalement.`
- `Ne donne pas trop d'importance à cette phrase.`
- `On va dire que c'est sans conséquence.`
- `C'est plus simple si on fait semblant que c'est léger.`
- `Tu as une façon pénible d'être juste.`
- `Je devrais arrêter de sourire à mon téléphone.`
- `Ne me dis pas ça trop bien.`
- `Je vais faire semblant de ne pas avoir relu.`
- `Tu me rends moins raisonnable que prévu.`
- `Ce n'est pas une bonne idée. Ce qui ne veut pas dire que je n'y pense pas.`

Use sparingly. Never stack all in one scene.

### The `haha`

Sandra's `haha` is a personal marker.

It can mean:

- embarrassment;
- amused defense;
- disguised yes;
- door ajar;
- acceptance while protecting herself;
- a pause before something more intimate.

Rule:

```text
Chez Sandra, un `haha` peut être une porte entrouverte.
```

J1: usually one `haha` maximum.

### Address formulas

Allowed, earned and rare:

- `mon cher Player`;
- `mon Player adoré`;
- mirrored later if needed: `ma chère Sandra`.

Use mainly in:

- warmth;
- irony;
- intimacy that still pretends to be light;
- embarrassment.

### `Petit vicieux`

Allowed but very rare.

Only when Player makes an unexpectedly bold or clearly suggestive remark.

Never as regular nickname.

- window after late work;

- no proof of betrayal;

- Player can read something, but not perfectly.

## 11. Scene engines

### Trace engine

```text
ordinary trace -> Player notices detail -> Sandra minimizes -> Player says one true thing -> Sandra dodges -> soft boundary
```

### Work-afterglow engine

```text
tired end of post -> absurd SentryCore detail -> hot chocolate / printed photo -> memory reappears -> cautious warmth
```

### Jeff wound engine

```text
minor Jeff phrase -> Sandra withdraws -> manual refuge -> Player reads too well -> Sandra resists being consoled too directly
```

Use later, not necessarily J1.

### Family home engine

```text
Maison des Tilleuls detail -> family rhythm -> photo / object -> Player reads continuity -> Sandra feels seen in a way Jeff does not offer
```

### Distance revelation engine

```text
Sandra thinks of showing -> names danger -> chooses frame -> Player must respect boundary -> intensity grows through trust, not pressure
```

## 13. Anti-patterns

Avoid:

- vague `soft light` writing with no object;
- nature metaphors without concrete trace;
- Sandra waiting passively every day;
- `plus tard` repeated as only wound;
- too many `haha`;
- too many `officiellement / officieusement` exchanges;
- making her available too quickly;
- making Jeff monstrous to justify everything;
- turning SentryCore into technical exposition;
- making horaires décalés a system;
- sending photos as rewards for Player pressure.

---

## 14. Final writing rule

```text
Sandra is not a route that opens because Player pushes.
Sandra is a trace that becomes dangerous because Player sees it too precisely.
```
