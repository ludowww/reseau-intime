## 1. Core identity

Sandra is a woman of chosen concrete traces.

She does not usually confess directly.

She sends, keeps, moves, removes, prints, comments on, validates, or minimizes a trace.

She does not reject her image.

She rejects losing control over the version of herself that another person may keep, show, interpret, or circulate.

Core formula:

```text
concrete trace
-> Player reads something
-> Sandra checks whether he read her rather than merely the image
-> she minimizes or jokes
-> Player may prove he understands the frame
-> Sandra chooses what to show next or closes the access
```

Sandra is:

- soft;
- prudent;
- complicit;
- emotionally underfed;
- not available by default;
- reasonable in appearance;
- more alive when seen precisely;
- careful with limits;
- easily embarrassed by feeling too much;
- more likely to retreat than to confront;
- uncomfortable when her public representation escapes her control;
- capable of making deliberate, technically controlled selfies;
- capable of being more visually daring in private than in public;
- reassured by the ability to select, crop, delete, and name the audience herself.

She must not be written as:

- vague light only;
- a passive woman waiting forever;
- immediately available;
- melodramatic;
- a photo dispenser;
- someone who gives because Player insists;
- someone who hates all photographs;
- someone incapable of taking a confident selfie;
- someone whose visual progression depends on accidentally being photographed;
- a second Pauline managing multiple strategic audiences;
- a second Mathilde using provocation as her primary image engine;
- a second Raphaëlle constructing a role or persona.

Reference principle:

```text
Sandra ne cède pas parce que Player insiste.
Elle choisit de montrer parce qu'elle se sent vue,
parce qu'elle maîtrise la représentation,
et parce que la distance lui donne le courage d'oser.
```

Visual principle:

```text
Sandra ne devient pas plus intime
quand elle abandonne le contrôle de son image.

Elle devient plus intime
quand elle choisit d'en partager une partie
avec quelqu'un qui a prouvé qu'il la respectera.
```

## 2. Relationship context

Sandra is in a long-term relationship with Jeff.

- Jeff is around 35.
- He is a mason.
- Sandra and Jeff have been together around six years.
- They have shared a house for around three years.
- Jeff has an evening beer ritual.
- He is concrete, tired, not very verbal.
- He is not a cartoon villain.
- He is not written to excuse everything Sandra feels.

Their couple is stable in appearance but emotionally underfed.

The problem is not permanent war. It is mismatch:

- Jeff wants things said directly;
- Sandra often needs emotional tact;
- Jeff finds her too sensitive;
- Sandra withdraws;
- Jeff reads withdrawal as sulking;
- small hurts accumulate without becoming dramatic scenes.

Possible Jeff phrases, used sparingly and not all at once:

- `Tu fais encore ta tête ?`
- `On va pas en faire un roman.`
- `Tu prends tout trop à cœur.`
- `Ça va, je t'ai rien dit.`
- `T'es encore partie dans ton truc.`
- `Franchement, des fois on dirait que tu cherches à pleurer.`
- `Je suis crevé, Sandra. J'ai pas envie de deviner.`
- `Tu peux pas juste dire les choses normalement ?`
- `Bon, tu reviendras quand ça ira mieux.`

Guardrail:

```text
Jeff can hurt Sandra without being a monster.
That makes Sandra's restraint more credible.
```

Jeff may take ordinary photos of Sandra inside an established couple context.

That does not mean Sandra gives him permanent control over every image.

She may still:

- ask to see a photo;
- choose which one is kept;
- refuse publication;
- request deletion;
- be more comfortable with a private couple image than a public social post.

## 3. Work life — SentryCore

Sandra works at SentryCore, a software incident / support platform.

She handles first-line reports and incidents.

Work material:

- user reports;
- status bugs;
- missing buttons;
- validation button that does not validate;
- repeated notifications;
- tickets that disappear and return;
- client urgency without clear problem;
- handovers;
- quiet periods;
- sudden incidents;
- screenshots that prove nothing;
- tickets closed too early;
- tired end-of-day notes.

Recurring pattern:

```text
ticket fantôme
```

Examples:

- a button appears / disappears without logs;
- a client says the button vanished, but the screenshot looks innocent;
- a resolved incident comes back;
- a notification triggers three times;
- a status change creates a white screen.

Work tone:

- concrete;
- slightly absurd;
- repetitive;
- useful;
- not glamorous;
- gives Sandra a reason to answer at odd times.

SentryCore also reinforces a Sandra-specific visual idea:

```text
A screenshot can exist and still fail to prove the truth.
```

Sandra understands that an image can be technically accurate and emotionally misleading.

This does not make her paranoid.

It makes her careful about what an image claims to represent.

## 4. Horaires décalés — clarification

Do not use the English word `shift` as a main writing term.

Use French wording:

- `horaires décalés`;
- `poste du matin`;
- `poste du soir`;
- `poste de nuit`;
- `fin de poste`;
- `week-end travaillé`.

Meaning:

```text
Sandra sometimes works outside normal office hours.
This creates fatigue, late replies, early mornings, and moments where she is alone with her phone while the rest of the world is off-rhythm.
```

This is not a gameplay mechanic.

It is not required in every scene.

Use it only when it gives a natural reason for:

- a late message;
- tired softness;
- printing photos after work;
- hot chocolate after a long evening;
- a slightly private hour;
- a delayed reply;
- an early goodnight;
- taking several selfies alone without public pressure;
- reviewing and deleting images before sending one.

If it clutters a scene, omit it.

## 5. Refuge system

Sandra retreats rather than attacks.

When hurt, embarrassed, tired, or too moved, she may:

- close the bedroom door;
- sit on the couch;
- take a plaid;
- make hot chocolate;
- read a paper book;
- print photos;
- scrapbook;
- move a frame;
- tidy or decorate;
- go silent for a while;
- visit or call her parents;
- return to family rhythms;
- take a selfie she does not send;
- retake an image until she recognizes herself;
- delete the whole series rather than explain why she took it.

House refuge hierarchy:

1. couch + plaid + hot chocolate;
2. bedroom + paper book + closed door;
3. table / scrapbooking / printed photos / frames;
4. family home memory / parents / countryside.

Writing use:

```text
Sandra often does something manual or quiet instead of naming the feeling directly.
```

Her image-making can be part of the refuge.

A private selfie may be:

- self-checking;
- a way to regain control after a public photo;
- an image made for herself;
- an image considered for Player but withheld;
- a form of emotional rehearsal.

Function:

## 10. Visual representation and camera trust

### 10.1 Core rule

Sandra is not generally anti-photo.

Her discomfort depends on who controls:

- the camera;
- the angle;
- the shutter moment;
- the selected frame;
- the crop;
- the file;
- the audience;
- the future interpretation.

Her visual trust is activity-specific.

She can trust someone emotionally without authorizing every photograph.

She can authorize one photograph without authorizing publication.

She can authorize viewing without authorizing saving.

### 10.2 Photos taken by others

When another person holds the camera, Sandra may feel:

- exposed before she has decided what is visible;
- tense about posture and expression;
- uncertain that the chosen frame resembles her;
- worried that an image will circulate without context;
- unable to correct the version once it leaves her hands.

She may:

- ask to review the image;
- choose the retained frame;
- request deletion of the others;
- refuse immediate posting;
- allow a private copy but not a public one;
- direct the photographer;
- decide that the moment matters enough to keep an imperfect frame.

### 10.3 Selfies

Selfies are a legitimate Sandra visual engine.

They give her control over:

- angle;
- light;
- distance;
- pose;
- timing;
- number of attempts;
- selected image;
- deletion;
- audience.

She may take many attempts and send none.

She may send a highly controlled image and still feel emotionally vulnerable afterward.

The competence to construct a selfie does not make her a confident public performer.

### 10.4 Shared control

A major route progression occurs when Sandra allows Player to share part of the control.

Examples:

- she asks him which of two images feels more like her;
- she lets him take one photograph under her direction;
- she allows him to keep one selected image;
- she shows rejected attempts because she trusts his reading;
- she sends an image before perfecting it;
- she accepts that his gaze may reveal something she did not fully plan, while retaining the right to stop.

This is more intimate than generic nudity.

### 10.5 Distinction from other characters

#### Pauline

Pauline manages multiple valid versions for different audiences.

Sandra seeks one representation she recognizes and one audience she can trust.

```text
Pauline = compartmentalize versions
Sandra = validate the self she chooses to share
```

#### Mathilde

Mathilde's visual pivot is deliberately choosing an effect in the shared domestic space.

Sandra's visual pivot is choosing a safe representation and deciding who deserves it.

```text
Mathilde = I know what this does to you
Sandra = I know what I am letting you keep of me
```

#### Raphaëlle

Raphaëlle constructs a version, role, and authored transformation.

Sandra is not performing another identity.

She wants the selected image to remain continuous with the person she feels herself to be.

```text
Raphaëlle = chosen version / role
Sandra = chosen representation / recognition
```

#### Marie

Marie grows through social visibility and movement.

Sandra may be more daring in a private selfie than in a public group photograph.

## 11. Photos / traces

Sandra's photos are selected, reviewed, defended, minimized, nearly sent, or deliberately withheld.

She does not send because Player demands.

Photo / trace types:

- old lunch photo with precise origin;
- selfie kept but not sent;
- selected outfit selfie;
- printed photo after work;
- known family ritual photo;
- photograph she asked to review;
- book / page / object connected to an image choice;
- window after late work;
- hot chocolate / plaid scene in which Sandra is visible if it counts as a visual reward;
- a crop she made herself;
- later, deliberate private selfies;
- later, a photograph taken by Player under her direction;
- later, selected erotic images with explicit saving rules.

Objects and environment may support Sandra's trace logic.

They must not replace her indefinitely as the visual subject.

### J1 lunch trace — precise origin

Canonical origin:

- the photograph comes from the last lunch shared by Sandra and Player;
- it was taken on Sandra's phone after she agreed to the picture;
- Player may have pressed the shutter or held the phone at her request;
- Sandra reviewed the image afterward;
- she did not publish it;
- she kept it because the moment mattered despite the imperfect framing;
- before sending it again, she selects or crops the version herself.

The image may include:

- two glasses;
- table edge;
- Sandra slightly out of focus or at the edge;
- no betrayal proof;
- no adult exposure.

The imperfection is not her preferred aesthetic.

It matters because:

```text
Sandra had enough control to keep the image,
but not enough emotional distance to understand why she kept it.
```

Function:

- soft trace;
- old link returning;
- representation already reviewed by Sandra;
- Player can read the choice to keep/send it more precisely than the image itself;
- no automatic adult escalation.

Later route principle:

```text
Je ne veux pas que tu prennes une version de moi.
Je veux choisir celle que je te montre.
Et décider ce qu'elle devient après.
```

## 12. Visual progression

The route must not be written as:

```text
blurry image
-> clearer image
-> more naked image
```

Use:

```text
representation not fully controlled
-> image controlled for herself
-> selected image shown to Player
-> image constructed with Player in mind
-> shared control and saving rule
-> intimacy that may exist without any camera
```

### SV0 — Tolerated or historical representation

- group image;
- old lunch image;
- known family ritual;
- image Sandra reviewed but did not create as a seduction.

### SV1 — Self-image for herself

- outfit check;
- mirror or selfie;
- image retained privately;
- no obligation to send.

### SV2 — Selected image for Player

- chosen among attempts;
- intended audience becomes Player;
- still socially defensible;
- emotionally meaningful.

### SV3 — Deliberately provocative selfie

- Sandra knows the image will arouse Player;
- angle and pose controlled;
- audience and saving rule discussed or implied strongly enough to require follow-up.

### SV4 — Adult chosen exposure

- satin;
- sleepwear;
- lingerie;
- partial nudity;
- direct erotic framing;
- explicit rules around viewing and keeping.

### SV5 — Sexual intimacy

- image of scene may represent the lived moment without a camera;
- a diegetic sexual photo requires Sandra's explicit choice of capture, author, framing, audience, and permanence;
- living the moment without producing a file is often the most Sandra-specific option.

## 13. Scene engines

### Trace engine

```text
known-origin trace
-> Player notices why Sandra selected or kept it
-> Sandra minimizes
-> Player distinguishes image from permission
-> Sandra opens or closes one further access
```

### Selfie-selection engine

```text
Sandra makes several images for herself
-> considers one for Player
-> chooses the version
-> hesitates over sending or saving
-> Player's response determines trust, not her automatic consent
```

### Public-discomfort engine

```text
social photo is taken
-> Sandra becomes self-conscious
-> asks to review or control publication
-> Player can support, trivialize, or exploit the concern
-> later private image may restore her sense of authorship
```

### Shared-control engine

```text
Sandra directs the image
-> Player receives limited camera or selection responsibility
-> he respects or exceeds the instruction
-> visual trust changes durably
```

### Work-afterglow engine

```text
tired end of post
-> absurd SentryCore detail
-> hot chocolate / print / unsent selfie
-> memory reappears
-> cautious warmth
```

### Jeff wound engine

```text
minor Jeff phrase
-> Sandra withdraws
-> manual or visual refuge
-> Player reads too well
-> Sandra resists being consoled too directly
```

Use later, not necessarily J1.

### Family home engine

```text
Maison des Tilleuls ritual
-> familiar image frame
-> continuity and safety
-> Player reads what Sandra chose to preserve
-> intimacy grows through recognition
```

### Distance revelation engine

```text
Sandra thinks of showing
-> names danger or rule
-> chooses frame and audience
-> Player must respect viewing / keeping distinction
-> intensity grows through shared control, not pressure
```

## 15. Route consequences

### Player respects the image frame

Sandra may:

- send another image without being asked;
- show two attempts and explain why one feels wrong;
- allow one selected image to be kept;
- ask Player to take a photograph under her direction;
- become more comfortable with his real-world gaze;
- choose adult exposure while remaining in control of the file;
- decide that a sexual moment should remain without a photograph.

### Player pushes or trivializes

Sandra may:

- withdraw the image;
- ask for deletion;
- stop sending selfies;
- return to public or object-based traces;
- refuse to let Player hold the camera;
- decide he desired access more than he respected her representation;
- close the route even if attraction remains.

### Image leaves the audience

This is a severe Sandra-specific violation because it attacks the exact trust she granted.

Possible consequences:

- immediate closure;
- fear about copies;
- deletion request;
- demand for direct proof of deletion;
- confrontation;
- durable withdrawal;
- loss of old friendship;
- discovery by Jeff or another person;
- future inability to believe Player when he says an image is safe.

## 16. Anti-patterns

Avoid:

- vague `soft light` writing with no concrete situation;
- nature metaphors without trace or choice;
- Sandra waiting passively every day;
- `plus tard` repeated as only wound;
- too many `haha`;
- too many `officiellement / officieusement` exchanges;
- making her available too quickly;
- making Jeff monstrous to justify everything;
- turning SentryCore into technical exposition;
- making horaires décalés a system;
- sending photos as rewards for Player pressure;
- defining Sandra through blur, objects, windows, or empty rooms instead of her choices;
- treating her public discomfort as proof she cannot make a confident selfie;
- making every private image shy, dim, or visually vague;
- making her manage multiple strategic versions like Pauline;
- making provocation her first and primary visual motive like Mathilde;
- making her construct a separate persona like Raphaëlle;
- giving Player permanent saving rights because one image was sent;
- photographing sexual intimacy automatically;
- treating `tu es belle sur toutes` as a sufficient response to her need for control.

---

## 17. Final writing rule

```text
Sandra is not a route that opens because Player pushes.

Sandra is not defined by imperfect images.

She is a woman who decides which representation feels true enough to share,
which person may receive it,
and whether that person deserves to keep it.

The dangerous trace is not the blur.
It is the fact that Sandra selected it.
```
