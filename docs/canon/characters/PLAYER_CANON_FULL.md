# Player — Full Character Canon

> Full current character canon.  
> Consolidates Player Concrete Profile V2, voice cards, post-etalon corrections, and current J1/J2 writing direction.  
> This is the first source for writing Player scenes.

## 1. Core identity

Player is discreet, observant, and often evasive.

He is not a blank avatar.

He is not a perfect seducer.

He is:

- around 32-34;
- in a five-year relationship with Marie;
- living with Marie for around three years;
- competent but tired;
- observant;
- ironic when embarrassed;
- slow to act;
- capable of precise perception;
- capable of missing the obvious human move;
- capable of love without active presence;
- playable because he is defined but not overdetermined.

Reference principle:

```text
Player observes life too much and enters it too late.
```

---

## 2. Relationship with Marie

Player loves Marie.

At the beginning, the couple is not dead.

The problem is not lack of love. The problem is passive delay.

Player has started treating stability as if it requires less attention.

He may:

- answer flatly;
- delay a response;
- joke instead of joining;
- sit on the couch too long;
- treat errands as small but fail to see their emotional function;
- reassure without acting;
- notice Marie's wound late;
- assume being there physically is enough.

What he must learn:

- loving is not only staying;
- noticing late is not enough;
- presence must sometimes happen before being requested;
- reassuring is weaker than joining;
- jealousy is not the same as desire;
- a couple is not maintained by default.

Writing guardrail:

```text
Player should make the reader feel that Marie is loved, but not actively enough.
```

---

## 3. Relationship with Sandra

Player is good at reading Sandra's traces.

That is precisely why the link is dangerous.

He notices:

- a smile in an old photo;
- a shift in tone;
- a soft dodge;
- the importance of a supposedly small object;
- the gap between Sandra's reasonableness and what she actually feels.

But he must not be perfect.

He can:

- overread;
- answer too neatly;
- pull back after saying something true;
- confuse usefulness with intimacy;
- enjoy being the one who sees;
- hide behind precision.

Sandra makes Player feel:

- useful;
- precise;
- seen as attentive;
- less ordinary;
- tempted by a confidence that does not demand public action yet.

Guardrail:

```text
Player must not become a magic reader of Sandra.
Sometimes he sees. Sometimes he guesses. Sometimes he protects himself by joking.
```

## 4. Work life

Player works as a functional project / product operations coordinator in a small software or digital-services company.

He is not a startup hero.

He is not a brilliant lone developer.

He deals with:

- meetings;
- specs;
- planning documents;
- vague priorities;
- bug reports;
- client feedback;
- dashboards;
- tickets;
- follow-ups;
- bad emails;
- cold coffee;
- too many browser tabs;
- delayed decisions;
- being tired from small unclear responsibilities.

Work colors his avoidance:

- a late email becomes an excuse;
- browser tabs mirror mental clutter;
- cold coffee signals fatigue;
- vague priorities mirror vague emotional priorities.

Use work lightly.

Do not turn scenes into office exposition.

Player's ordinary life matters.

Use:

- forgotten bread;
- couch;
- simple cooking;
- quiet walks;
- half-watched series;
- books bought faster than read;
- bad organization;
- phone too close;
- laptop nearby;
- cold coffee;
- small photos sent as ordinary traces;

- tomatoes / bread / cheese with Marie;

These anchors make him playable and real.

## 6. Occasional chess

Player is an occasional chess player.

This is a color, not an identity.

He is not a chess genius.

Function:

- he anticipates patterns;
- he calculates too much;
- he waits for the right move;
- he misses the simple human move;
- he sometimes loses a won position.

Usable lines:

- `J'ai perdu une partie gagnante. Très moi.`
- `Je réfléchis trop pour un type qui finit quand même par jouer le mauvais coup.`
- `Je suis fort pour anticiper aux échecs. Beaucoup moins en couple.`
- `Je crois que j'attends toujours le bon coup. Mauvaise méthode.`
- `Je suis en train de calculer au lieu de parler. Très mauvais signe.`

Use rarely. Do not make every Player scene chess-coded.

## 7. Humor and defense

Player uses humor when:

- embarrassed;
- late;
- moved;
- jealous;
- caught;
- too honest too quickly;
- unsure how to answer;
- trying to soften a truth.

His humor should not always be clever.

It can be:

- dry;
- slightly lame;
- too late;
- banal;
- self-aware;
- a half-dodge;
- a way to survive discomfort.

If Player sounds too polished, flatten him.

Good Player texture:

```text
Player : je recommence
Player : pas terrible comme réponse
```

## 8. Voice

Player messages should often be short.

He can write:

- `ok`
- `oui`
- `bon`
- `pas faux`
- `j'ai vu`
- `désolé`
- `je recommence`
- `je vais dire un truc con`
- `trop tard`
- `mauvaise idée`
- `un peu`
- `beaucoup même`
- `j'ai souri. voilà.`

Typical modes:

### Esquive courte

- `question piège`
- `j'adore mourir simplement`
- `je vais répondre prudemment`

### Observation

- `t'as changé un détail`
- `pas énorme`
- `mais j'ai vu`

### Mauvaise foi

- `je suis calme`
- `intérieurement non`
- `mais extérieurement ça passe`

### Flirt maladroit

- `je pourrais dire un truc intelligent`
- `mais là j'ai surtout envie de te revoir`

Use flirt with care and only when earned.

### Honnête sec

- `oui`
- `j'ai attendu ton message`
- `je sais`

### Limite

- `non`
- `pas comme ça`
- `j'ai envie, mais pas comme ça`

### Retour vers Marie

- `faut que je parle à Marie`
- `pas pour fuir`
- `justement parce que je suis en train de pas fuir assez`

## 9. Player as choice subject

Player choices should not be abstract meta labels only.

Even if the UI choice label is short, the resulting Player message must feel like a real message he sends.

Bad:

```text
Dire qu'il va essayer d'être plus présent.
```

Better:

```text
Player : je vais essayer d'être là pour de vrai
Player : pas juste dans la pièce
```

Player must remain visible in conversations.

He should not disappear behind long monologues from other characters.

## 10. Relationship functions with other characters

### Marie

Marie forces the question:

```text
Are you joining the life you already have?
```

### Sandra

Sandra forces the question:

```text
Are you noticing because you care, or because being the one who notices feels good?
```

### Mathilde

Mathilde forces domestic ambiguity.

She makes Player feel that the home itself can become unstable.

### Pauline

Pauline forces speed, wit, and risk.

She can make Player feel clever, but also compromised.

### Raphaëlle

Raphaëlle forces clarity.

She sees the professional / practical version of Player and refuses to become a hiding place.

### Nico

Nico names Player's jealousy and bodily reaction more directly than Player would.

He reveals the male excitement Player wants to moralize away.

## 11. Scene engines

### Passive delay engine

```text
small request -> Player delays / jokes -> other person notices quality of response -> Player can correct or drift
```

### Observation engine

```text
trace / detail -> Player notices -> he says too much or too little -> reaction proves whether he read well
```

### Return-to-Marie engine

```text
outside tension -> Marie asks for ordinary presence -> Player must choose a small real act instead of a big explanation
```

### Shame / honesty engine

```text
Player dodges -> realizes dodge is visible -> gives one short honest sentence -> may then retreat
```

### Jealousy mirror engine

```text
Marie visible elsewhere -> Nico or social gaze names it -> Player feels physical reaction -> he must distinguish love, desire, control
```

Use later, not J1.

## 12. J1 usage

J1 Player should:

- respond to Marie as part of shared life;
- be able to buy bread / walk / return attention;
- receive Sandra's trace without route lock;
- notice the old photo if chosen;
- stay imperfect;
- not become a perfect seducer;
- not choose Sandra over Marie;
- end with Marie as emotional center.

Good J1 Player texture:

- short replies;
- flat option;
- delayed option;
- one precise Sandra reading if chosen;
- concrete return to Marie.

---

## 13. Anti-patterns

Avoid:

- empty avatar;
- perfect seducer;
- constant cleverness;
- noble guilt speech;
- always saying the exact right thing;
- always misbehaving in the same way;
- having other characters diagnose him while he contributes nothing;
- turning his work into exposition;
- overusing chess metaphors;
- treating his observation as moral virtue.

---

## 14. Final writing rule

```text
Player can notice the right thing and still fail to do the simple thing.
That gap is the engine.
```
