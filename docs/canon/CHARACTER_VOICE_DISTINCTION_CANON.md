# Character Voice Distinction Canon

> Cross-character dialogue guardrail for all current and future source packs.  
> Complements the full character canon files; it does not replace them.  
> Documentation only.

## 1. Purpose

`Réseau Intime` depends on characters producing the excitement through their own psychology, history, habits, and voice.

A dialogue pass fails when every character becomes:

- equally witty;
- equally precise;
- equally administrative;
- equally fluent in the same metaphors;
- a vehicle for the author's favorite phrasing.

```text
A recognizable line must come from a recognizable life.
```

The full character canon remains the first source. This document prevents strong lexical markers from leaking across the ensemble during modular writing.

---

## 2. Core rule

```text
Voice markers are not communal props.
```

A phrase pattern may be shared only when:

- the context literally requires it;
- the characters have a credible shared history that explains the echo;
- the borrowing itself is noticed or meaningful;
- it remains exceptional rather than becoming the new generic voice.

A clever line is not automatically a good line for every character.

---

## 3. Legal vocabulary ownership

Mathilde's legal-protection work gives her a specific light legal seasoning.

Compatible Mathilde language includes, at controlled dosage:

```text
objection
irrecevable
dossier
pièce à conviction
preuve insuffisante
vice de procédure
je plaide...
```

Recommended dosage:

```text
ordinary scene = zero or one legal turn
work-centered scene = up to two or three when useful
earned complicity scene = one or two maximum
adult scene = legal vocabulary normally decreases
```

The legal register is seasoning, not a cast-wide comedy template.

### 3.1 Non-Mathilde rule

Marie, Sandra, Pauline, Nico, Raphaëlle, and Player may use a legal word when it is literally normal in the situation.

Examples:

- a real work file may be called a `dossier`;
- a bank or contract issue may be named literally;
- a real proof may be discussed as evidence;
- a character may quote Mathilde once.

They must not borrow legal vocabulary as recurring flirt or humor shorthand.

Reject non-Mathilde lines built around:

```text
base contractuelle
verdict
accusé
objection
je classe le dossier
j'enregistre la dette sans intérêts
pièce à conviction
```

unless the literal scene context makes the term unavoidable.

## 4. Character-specific voice ownership

## 4.1 Marie

Primary sources:

- shared life;
- food;
- errands;
- La Verrière;
- movement;
- social visibility;
- practical teasing;
- concrete action before reassurance.

Marie can move quickly from funny to serious.

She should not sound like:

- Mathilde doing legal wordplay;
- Raphaëlle defining a frame;
- Pauline managing an audience;
- a therapist explaining the couple.

Good Marie principle:

```text
She makes the ordinary life worth joining,
then notices whether Player actually joins it.
```

## 4.2 Sandra

Primary sources:

- a concrete trace;
- minimization;
- soft embarrassment;
- SentryCore absurdity;
- hot chocolate, paper books, quiet refuge;
- chosen distance;
- a boundary that does not need aggression.

Sandra's precision is fragile and defensive, not procedural authority.

She should not sound like:

- Mathilde's legal sketch;
- Pauline's controlled test;
- Raphaëlle's explicit framing;
- a generic poetic woman made of soft light.

## 4.3 Mathilde

Primary sources:

- speed;
- correction;
- bad faith;
- embarrassment;
- image control;
- ordinary sensuality;
- work and family;
- occasional legal seasoning.

Mathilde may begin a sentence confidently, correct herself, close the subject, then reopen it.

Her legal vocabulary must not replace:

- humor;
- vulnerability;
- work competence;
- loyalty to Marie;
- desire;
- ordinary speech.

## 4.4 Pauline

Primary sources:

- dry control;
- timing;
- audience;
- public and private versions;
- practical social competence;
- a line that appears closed and then returns;
- the temptation to separate incompatible truths.

Pauline can sound organized without sounding juridical.

Her banking vocabulary is literal work color, not her universal flirt grammar.

She should not borrow Mathilde's `objection / dossier / accusé` rhythm.

## 4.5 Nico

Primary sources:

- food;
- service;
- chairs and rooms;
- social rhythm;
- work at L'Annexe;
- direct ordinary language;
- humor that can stop when the room becomes quiet.

Nico does not need administrative cleverness to sound intelligent.

His future adult directness comes from saying desire plainly, not from legal or banking metaphors.

## 4.6 Raphaëlle

Primary sources:

- process;
- one exact detail;
- a simple question;
- selected information;
- calm spacing;
- a precise invitation or limit;
- the difference between work and a chosen private version.

Raphaëlle can be clear without turning every sentence into a contract.

Avoid default lines such as:

```text
base contractuelle
clause
accord validé
```

Her clarity comes from observation and decision, not borrowed legal vocabulary.

## 4.7 Player

Primary sources:

- short replies;
- dry humor;
- embarrassment;
- imperfect honesty;
- observation;
- occasional work fatigue;
- the gap between seeing and acting.

Player may mirror another character once in a credible exchange.

He must not become a universal stylist who instantly speaks every character's strongest idiom.

## 5. Vocabulary overlap rules

Some words legitimately belong to more than one person. Meaning and rhythm must still differ.

### `version`

- Pauline: which audience receives which version;
- Raphaëlle: which chosen self or authored frame is being shown;
- Mathilde: which image of herself she can still deny or control;
- other characters: occasional ordinary use only.

### `preuve`

- documentation: neutral state/trace vocabulary;
- Mathilde dialogue: possible legal joke;
- Pauline: evidence and mutual implication;
- Nico: what an image or alibi proves;
- other dialogue: use only when context earns it.

### `dossier`

- literal work file: available to relevant jobs;
- humorous recurring marker: Mathilde only by default.

### `cadre`

- Raphaëlle: explicit relational or role structure;
- Pauline: image framing or compartment boundaries;
- Nico: named shared-gaze or consent arrangement later;
- early ordinary dialogue: avoid spreading it everywhere.

## 6. Anti-pattern examples

### Bad leakage

```text
Marie : J'enregistre la proposition au dossier.
Raphaëlle : Très bonne base contractuelle.
Nico : Je note la dette sans intérêts.
Pauline : Objection.
Sandra : Pièce à conviction.
```

The lines may be individually understandable. Together they erase the cast.

### Better differentiation

```text
Marie : Je garde l'heure, pas la bonne intention.
Raphaëlle : C'est presque une promenade normale. On va tester.
Nico : Si tu me plantes, je mange ta part.
Pauline : Je prends la réponse. Malgré l'absence de dessert.
Sandra : Ne transforme pas ça en grande vérité.
Mathilde : Je la garde pour le dossier.
```

Each line now grows from a different person.

## 7. Modular-scene review test

Before a scene card is approved, review every voiced line against these questions:

```text
1. Could another principal character say the line unchanged?
2. Is a profession-specific marker leaking into the whole cast?
3. Is every character equally witty or equally articulate?
4. Does the line use the character's objects, work, routines, wound, and defense?
5. Does Player sound like himself rather than a perfect imitation?
6. Is the character allowed to be banal, tired, awkward, or briefly inarticulate?
7. Does intensity preserve voice rather than replace it?
```

A scene fails voice review when name substitution leaves it essentially unchanged.

---

## 8. Maintenance rule

When a voice correction changes exact dialogue:

1. update the exact-line source pack;
2. update the matching scene card if its engine or posture changes;
3. update the temporal map only if delivery changes;
4. update the version report and PR review note;
5. do not rewrite identity canon unless the correction changes the person rather than the line.

```text
Distinct psychology requires distinct language.
```

---

## 9. Final rule

```text
Mathilde may season a line with law.
Pauline controls versions.
Raphaëlle names what is being chosen.
Nico makes the room and the desire concrete.
Sandra protects what she almost revealed.
Marie makes shared life move.
Player sees, jokes, delays, and sometimes finally acts.

Do not make them all sound like the same clever writer.
```
