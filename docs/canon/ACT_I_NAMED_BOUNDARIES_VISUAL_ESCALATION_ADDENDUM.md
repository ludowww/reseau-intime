# Act I Named Boundaries — Visual-First Pornographic Escalation Addendum — V0.93

> Product correction after review of the first V0.93 source-pack draft.
> This addendum is authoritative for visual cadence, erotic progression, route readability, photo rewards, visual risk, and possible derailments.
> It supersedes every V0.93 clause that says no new image is required, no route transition is allowed, or visual opportunities must remain entirely public and neutral.
> Documentation only: it does not create runtime, JSON, GDScript, tests, final assets, merges, or tags.

## 1. Product correction

`Réseau Intime` is not a text-first romantic game with occasional illustrations.

It is an adult visual narrative game in which:

```text
photos are the primary reward loop
messages create anticipation
choices determine access, framing, risk, and escalation
the gallery makes route progression visible
pornographic content is a promised destination, not an embarrassed exception
```

The story must remain credible because credibility makes the visual and sexual progression stronger.

Credibility must not be used to keep the game permanently soft, neutral, cautious, or visually empty.

```text
The player should want to continue because he wants to see
what each woman will show next,
why she shows it,
to whom,
and how far that route can evolve.
```

## 2. Hard daily photo rule

Every playable day must unlock at least:

```text
3 distinct female-focused photos
```

This is a minimum, not a target maximum.

Recommended daily budget:

```text
3 guaranteed visual unlocks
+ 0 to 3 route-conditional visual unlocks
```

Recommended strong-route day:

```text
4 to 6 photos
```

Across the three-day V0.93 wave:

```text
minimum production scope = 9 distinct unlockable images
target scope = 12 to 18 images including route variants
```

A photo set counts only when each frame is individually viewable and unlockable in the gallery.

A single composite image containing three thumbnails does not satisfy the rule unless the three frames can be opened separately.

## 5. Visual intensity ladder

The gallery and route design should support a readable escalation.

### V1 — Elegant

- attractive outfit;
- full body visible;
- controlled public or ordinary framing;
- no uncertainty that the woman is desirable.

### V2 — Sexy

- body emphasis is deliberate;
- legs, waist, cleavage, back, fitted clothing, or pose become central;
- image is still socially defensible.

### V3 — Provocative

- the woman knows the image will arouse the intended viewer;
- private angle, alternate crop, lingerie glimpse, body-conscious pose, or deliberate invitation;
- route opportunity becomes clear.

### V4 — Erotic adult image

- lingerie, nudity, explicit body exposure, masturbatory framing, or clearly sexual pose;
- intended audience and saving rule matter;
- no sex act required yet.

### V5 — Pornographic scene or image

- explicit nudity, genital focus, masturbation, oral sex, penetration, group activity, voyeuristic scene, roleplay sex, or another clearly pornographic act;
- character route, consent frame, risk, and aftermath remain distinct.

V0.93 should guarantee V1–V2 images every day.

At least one selected route should be able to reach:

```text
V3 guaranteed
or a first V4 teaser
```

V0.93 does not need to contain full V5 intercourse to prove that the game is pornographic.

It must make V5 feel like a credible near-future reward for the route the player chooses.

## 7. Route-readability rule

The player should easily understand what a risky choice may unlock.

Do not hide every opportunity behind neutral wording.

The choice text itself should communicate:

- desire;
- curiosity;
- jealousy;
- request for a private version;
- willingness to be watched;
- refusal;
- willingness to keep a secret;
- interest in sharing the gaze;
- attraction to a specific fantasy.

Avoid internal route labels such as `Mathilde Route`.

But do not flatten every choice into polite ambiguity.

Good adult-game choice logic:

```text
Ask for the more private version.
Tell her exactly what the pose does to Player.
Let the public image remain public.
Ask Nico what he wants to see.
Refuse to circulate the image.
Encourage Marie to wear the dress where others will see her.
Ask Pauline whether there is another crop.
```

The player should see the opportunity and the possible danger before clicking.

- choosing an outfit;
- getting ready;
- a mirror;

- a request for another version;

The conversation should not spend twenty messages justifying why a sexy woman sent a sexy photo in an adult game.


The visual should carry part of the story.

Characters should not all deliver therapy-like explanations before showing anything.
