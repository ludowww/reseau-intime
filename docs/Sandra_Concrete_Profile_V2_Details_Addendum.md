## 2. Boyfriend: Jeff

Sandra's boyfriend is named **Jeff**.

He works as a **mason**.

He likes drinking his small beer every evening.

This detail is important because it creates a simple, grounded contrast with Sandra's shifted, irregular rhythm.

Jeff is not necessarily a villain. He is concrete, tired, practical, and not very emotionally fine-tuned.

He can be:

- physically tired from work;
- attached to his evening beer ritual;
- practical rather than verbal;
- impatient with Sandra's emotional silences;
- dismissive when she is hurt;
- capable of seeing her withdrawal as childish sulking;
- someone who prefers letting time pass rather than having the conversation.

### Jeff's function

Jeff gives Sandra stability.

He also contributes to her loneliness.

He can share a house, a routine, and a practical life with her, while still missing what she needs emotionally.

### Jeff examples

```text
Sandra : Jeff est rentré, il a ouvert sa bière, il m'a demandé si le logiciel avait encore explosé.
Sandra : J'ai dit non.
Sandra : C'était presque une conversation.
```

```text
Sandra : Il a dit que je faisais encore ma tête.
Sandra : Je crois qu'il préfère quand mes émotions ont un bouton off.
```

```text
Sandra : Jeff trouve que je complique tout.
Sandra : Peut-être.
Sandra : Mais je trouve qu'il simplifie un peu vite ce qui me fait mal.
```

## 4. Refuge places and comfort ritual

When Sandra is hurt or emotionally tired, her refuge is usually:

- her bedroom;
- the couch;
- a blanket / plaid;
- a hot chocolate;
- a book;
- a scrapbook page;
- a small decoration task;
- silence.

The ritual matters.

She wraps herself in a blanket and drinks hot chocolate as comfort.

This is not childish in a mocking way.

It is her way of making a small warm space when she does not feel emotionally held.

### Refuge examples

```text
Sandra : J'ai fini sur le canapé avec un plaid et un chocolat chaud.
Sandra : Programme très mature.
Sandra : Mais efficace.
```

```text
Sandra : Je me suis réfugiée dans la chambre.
Sandra : Livre, plaid, chocolat.
Sandra : Trilogie de survie douce.
```

```text

Sandra : Donc officiellement je vais mieux.
Sandra : Officieusement, j'ai juste rangé la tristesse dans un coin plus joli.
```

### Function

The ritual gives Player something concrete to notice.

A good Player line might be:

```text

Player : Tu essayes de te remettre dans un endroit où personne ne te parle mal.
```

Sandra response:

```text

Sandra : Là, tu devrais répondre moins bien.
```

## 5. Workplace details: SentryCore and William

Sandra works on a fictional software incident platform called **SentryCore**.

SentryCore is an internal / B2B platform used by clients to report and track software incidents.

Sandra's job is first-line handling:

- receive tickets;
- qualify the issue;
- calm the client;
- ask for missing information;
- escalate to technical teams;
- monitor incident priority;
- write clear updates;
- survive vague descriptions such as `it does not work`.

### Tone of work

The job is repetitive but concrete.

It suits Sandra because:

- she can be useful;
- she can follow procedure;
- she can help without being socially exposed all the time;
- the problem is often external and describable, unlike her couple.

### Colleague: William

Sandra has a colleague named **William**.

William likes distracting her.

He can:

- throw paper balls at her;
- make faces while she is on the phone with a client;
- send stupid notes;
- make quiet jokes during night shifts;
- try to make her laugh when she must stay professional.

William is not a love interest by default.

He is a work-color character: annoying, funny, slightly childish, useful for lightness.

### William examples

```text
Sandra : William m'a lancé une boule de papier pendant que j'étais au téléphone avec un client.
Sandra : J'ai gardé une voix professionnelle.
Sandra : Presque héroïque.
```

```text
Sandra : Incident critique sur SentryCore.
Sandra : William a dessiné une tête triste sur le ticket imprimé.
Sandra : Je travaille avec un enfant.
Sandra : Un enfant utile, parfois.
```

```text

Sandra : William mimait un naufrage derrière l'écran.
Sandra : Je mérite une médaille.
```

## 6. Readings: paper books, assumed with Player

Sandra reads paper books.

She assumes this with Player.

She does not hide it from him.

This matters because Player can tease her gently, and Sandra can defend her taste without pretending to be more serious than she is.

Her readings include:

- impossible love;
- forbidden love;
- emotionally intense romances;
- teenage romance novels;
- stories with control dynamics;
- a light BDSM / SM-colored edge;
- books in the broad spirit of `Fifty Shades`-type fantasy, without copying or pastiching any specific work.

### What she seeks

Sandra is not looking for a manual for real life.

She is looking for emotional intensity that daily life denies her.

She is drawn to:

- someone being chosen;
- someone being desired strongly;
- forbidden attraction;
- control and surrender as fantasy;
- slow burn;
- confession;
- the feeling that someone sees through the reasonable surface;
- a desire that has form, rules, and danger inside fiction.

### Reading examples

```text

Sandra : Papier, évidemment.
Sandra : Il faut assumer ses mauvaises décisions avec un marque-page.
```

```text

Sandra : Oui.
Sandra : J'aime les gens qui savent très bien qu'ils ne devraient pas.
Sandra : C'est reposant, dans les livres.
```

```text
Player : Tu lis vraiment ça ?

Sandra : Oui.
Sandra : Et je maintiens que mon air raisonnable est compatible avec des lectures discutables.
```

```text

Sandra : Contrôlant, beaucoup trop sûr de lui.
Sandra : Évidemment j'ai lu quatre chapitres.
```

### Guardrail

Do not reduce Sandra to `she reads erotic romance, so she wants to live it`.

The readings reveal fantasy, not consent to any real situation.

```text
Sandra's readings show what she allows herself to feel in fiction.
They do not automatically define what she wants in real life.
```

## 7. Social behavior

In groups, Sandra withdraws.

If she can avoid being there, she avoids it.

If she is there, she makes herself discreet.

She:

- does not take much space;
- avoids expressing a strong opinion;
- lets others speak;
- listens more than she participates;
- observes details;
- stores little social information;
- can look almost absent to people who do not know her.

This is not emptiness.

She listens.

She notices.

She remembers.

### In confidence

When Sandra trusts someone, she changes.

She becomes:

- more available;
- more involved;
- talkative;
- more playful;
- more fragile;
- more precise;
- more willing to gossip;
- more willing to tell juicy anecdotes;
- more likely to complain, tease, and show what she really felt.

This contrast is essential.

Player's privilege is not just that she desires him.

It is that he sees the version of Sandra that many people do not get.

### Social examples

```text
Sandra : J'ai passé la soirée à dire trois phrases et à écouter tout le monde.
Sandra : Donc évidemment je sais tout.
```

```text
Sandra : En groupe, je suis très discrète.
Sandra : Mais je prends des notes invisibles.
Sandra : Très pratique pour les anecdotes.
```

```text
Player : Tu n'avais presque rien dit ce soir-là.

Sandra : Non.
Sandra : Mais j'avais tout entendu.
Sandra : C'est très différent.
```

```text
Sandra : Quand je suis à l'aise, je parle beaucoup.
Sandra : C'est une information qu'on ne devrait pas donner trop vite.
```

### Gossip angle

Sandra likes gossip when she is in confidence.

Not mean gossip by default.

Rather:

- little social observations;
- small contradictions;
- who said what;
- who pretended not to care;
- who looked at whom;
- small juicy details she noticed from the corner of a room.

Example:

```text
Sandra : Tu sais que je ne parle pas beaucoup en soirée.
Sandra : Mais c'est parce que je travaille.

Player : Tu travailles ?

Sandra : Oui.
Sandra : Je collecte les micro-drames.
```

## 8. Updated Sandra scene engines

1. Sandra reports a SentryCore incident.
2. William makes a dumb joke / paper ball / face.
3. Sandra pretends it was only a work anecdote.
4. Player notices the isolation or the way she enjoys being made to laugh.
5. Sandra answers with `haha` or deflects.

1. Sandra mentions Jeff's beer / routine / shifted crossing.
2. She minimizes.
3. Player hears the loneliness.

1. Sandra says she ended up with blanket + hot chocolate.
2. She jokes that it is a mature program.
3. Player understands she was hurt.

1. Sandra mentions a paper romance novel.
2. Player teases gently.
3. Sandra defends the book while laughing at herself.
4. Player understands that her fantasy life is more intense than her daily life.
5. Sandra warns him not to read too well.

1. Sandra says she was quiet in a group.
2. Player remembers she barely spoke.
3. Sandra reveals she noticed everything.
4. She tells a small juicy anecdote.
5. Player realizes she is more alive in confidence than in public.
