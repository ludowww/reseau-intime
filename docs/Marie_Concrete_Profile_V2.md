# Marie — Concrete Profile V2

> Documentation-only writing addendum.  
> Complements `docs/Character_Playable_Voice_Cards.md`, `docs/Character_Voice_Color_Addendum.md`, and `docs/Character_Voice_Post_Etalons_Addendum.md`.  
> Purpose: make Marie as scenarizable as Sandra for J1/J2 and early-route writing.  
> No runtime, narrative JSON, tests, assets, or playable content are changed.

## 1. Core diagnosis

Marie already has a strong voice and dramatic function.

The existing voice card establishes that she is:

- Player's partner;
- alive, sociable, funny, and active;
- not only fragile or jealous;
- the emotional center of the couple;
- someone who wants presence, not mechanical reassurance;
- capable of reconquest, provocation, and clear limits.

The missing layer is not voice.

The missing layer is concrete daily material.

```text
Marie must not exist only as the woman Player risks losing.
Marie must exist as the life Player risks no longer living with her.
```

---

## 2. Signature V2

Marie is the woman of shared life and social energy.

She wants the couple to remain alive:

- going out;
- laughing;
- eating together;
- seeing friends;
- making plans;
- being seen by each other;
- being chosen in ordinary moments;
- not turning into two people sitting in the same room with different inner lives.

She can be funny and serious in the same exchange.

She can be wounded without becoming passive.

Reference line:

```text
Marie : Tu es avec moi ou juste dans la pièce ?
```

V2 reference line:

```text
Marie : Je ne veux pas qu'on devienne un couple qui se raconte sa journée comme un compte rendu.
Marie : Je veux encore qu'on ait quelque chose à se dire.
```

## 3. Work / daily rhythm

Marie works as a **communications and small-events coordinator** in a local cultural / associative structure.

This job fits her because it gives her:

- people;
- invitations;
- small events;
- posters;
- social planning;
- last-minute problems;
- group photos;
- public smiles;
- a reason to know many people;
- a reason to be visible without being superficial.

She is not famous or glamorous.

Her work is concrete and social:

- preparing a small exhibition opening;
- coordinating a local event;
- sending invitations;
- checking posters;
- dealing with a printer problem;
- calling a caterer;
- organizing a room;
- managing people who answer too late.

### Work examples

```text
Marie : J'ai passé vingt minutes à expliquer que "avant vendredi" ne voulait pas dire "vendredi à 18h12".
Marie : Je vais devenir une affiche agressive.
```

```text
Marie : L'imprimeur a inversé deux logos.
Marie : Le traiteur a oublié les serviettes.
Marie : Mais j'ai des boucles d'oreilles très solides, donc je tiens encore debout.
```

```text
Marie : On a une petite expo jeudi.
Marie : Je dis petite pour ne pas paniquer.
Marie : C'est évidemment trop tard.
```

## 4. Social world

- restaurants;

- walks after dinner;
- laughing too loudly for five minutes;

Marie does not go out only to provoke Player.

She goes out because it is part of how she feels alive.

## 6. Relationship wound

Marie's wound is not only jealousy.

Her wound is feeling that she is becoming alone inside the couple.

She fears that Player is still physically present but emotionally elsewhere.

She does not want to beg for attention.

She wants to be joined.

She wants Player to participate in the life they are supposed to share.

### What hurts her

Marie is hurt when Player:

- answers mechanically;
- stays vague;
- notices too late;
- watches from the side;
- lets her carry the mood;
- treats her energy as noise;
- becomes absent while sitting next to her;
- only reacts when another man notices her.

### Wound examples

```text
Marie : Je ne vais pas courir derrière toi dans notre propre vie.
```

```text
Marie : Je ne veux pas être la seule à tenir l'ambiance.
Marie : C'est lourd.
Marie : Et ça me rend moins drôle.
```

```text
Marie : Le problème, ce n'est pas que tu ne m'aimes plus.
Marie : Le problème, c'est que parfois tu m'aimes comme si j'étais déjà acquise.
```

## 7. How Marie reacts when hurt

Marie does not only ask Player to put his phone away.

When hurt, she acts.

She can:

- shorten her replies;
- stop explaining;
- go out anyway;
- dress up;
- accept being seen by others;
- become brighter elsewhere;
- post or send a photo with a function;
- let Nico or social attention reveal what Player has neglected;
- impose a direct conversation later.

Her pain becomes action.

That action can be healthy, provocative, or dangerous depending on context.

### Hurt response examples

```text
Marie : Ok.
Marie : Message reçu.
Marie : Je vais arrêter de faire l'ambiance toute seule ce soir.
```

```text
Marie : J'ai mis la robe noire.
Marie : Tu peux remarquer maintenant.
Marie : Ou apprendre par témoignage extérieur.
```

```text
Marie : Je ne fais pas ça pour te punir.
Marie : Je fais ça parce que je refuse de devenir invisible dans mon propre couple.
```

## 8. Reconquest and provocation

Marie can provoke Player, but provocation is not her only mode.

She can also invite him back into the couple.

Her reconquest energy is:

- playful;
- warm;
- challenging;
- physical but not only sexual;
- socially alive;
- sometimes wounded;
- sometimes proud.

### Reconquest examples

```text
Marie : Message reçu.
Marie : Maintenant message à prouver.
```

```text
Marie : J'ai encore envie qu'on soit une bonne idée.
Marie : Mais il va falloir participer.
```

```text
Marie : Tu viens marcher avec moi ?
Marie : Pas pour régler toute notre vie.
Marie : Juste pour arrêter de la laisser moisir sur le canapé.
```

### Provocation guardrail

Marie must not become someone who provokes only to hurt Player.

Her provocation should usually hide one of three needs:

1. be seen;
2. be joined;
3. regain power when she feels humiliated.

## 9. Nico's function around Marie

Nico is a revealer.

He notices what Player does not.

Marie does not become `stolen` by Nico.

She discovers that she can still be noticed elsewhere.

This can be validating, dangerous, funny, or uncomfortable.

### Nico / Marie examples

```text
Marie : Nico a remarqué ma robe avant toi.
Marie : Je ne dis pas ça pour te faire une scène.
Marie : Je dis ça parce que c'est factuel.
Marie : Et un peu vexant.
```

```text
Marie : Il m'a fait rire.
Marie : Ça ne devrait pas être un événement.
Marie : Mais apparemment, ce soir, ça en est un.
```

### Guardrail

Nico must not erase Marie's agency.

Marie is not an object transferred from one man's gaze to another.

She is a woman realizing that being seen changes how she stands inside her own couple.

## 10. Pauline, Mathilde, Sandra around Marie

### Pauline

Pauline is Marie's friend and social mirror.

She can:

- encourage Marie to go out;
- take photos;
- notice when Marie is pretending to be fine;
- create social opportunities;
- tease Player indirectly;
- later become a more ambiguous channel.

Marie trusts Pauline, but Pauline's private route with Player must never erase that she is first tied to Marie.

### Mathilde

Mathilde is Marie's cousin.

She brings:

- family proximity;
- domestic access;
- teasing;
- small chaos;
- an intimate but risky presence around the couple.

Marie can complain about Mathilde, protect her, laugh with her, and miss signs because family familiarity makes things look harmless.

### Sandra

Sandra is not Marie's closest friend.

They know each other from farther away.

Marie may see Sandra as gentle, quiet, not threatening at first.

That makes the Player / Sandra intimacy more dangerous because it begins in emotional precision rather than social rivalry.

## 11. Photos Marie

Marie photos must always say something about the couple.

Photo functions:

- **life trace**: she shares an ordinary moment and wants Player inside it;
- **presence test**: will Player notice a detail?
- **social proof**: she is seen elsewhere;
- **reconquest**: she chooses to be desirable and wants Player to respond;
- **power regained**: she stops waiting quietly;
- **hurt signal**: the photo is bright because she refuses to look abandoned.

### Photo examples

```text
Marie : [photo: shoes by the door, dress visible in mirror edge]
Marie : Je suis prête.
Marie : Toi mentalement, on verra.
```

```text
Marie : [photo: tomatoes, bread missing]
Marie : Couple en crise logistique.
Marie : Priorité pain.
```

```text
Marie : [photo: group table, her smile, Nico blurred in background]
Marie : Ambiance correcte.
Marie : Je te laisse trouver le détail agaçant.
```

### Guardrail

Marie photos are not decorative.

They must test, invite, reveal, or shift power.

## 12. Scene engines

### Domestic engine

1. Marie sends a domestic detail: tomatoes, bread, laundry, couch, dinner.
2. Player answers too flat or finally joins.
3. Marie tests whether he is really present.
4. The ordinary moment becomes couple state.

### Going-out engine

1. Marie proposes or announces an outing.
2. Player hesitates / lags / hides.
3. Marie teases him as `l'ermite`.
4. If he stays absent, she goes socially alive without him.

### Hurt-shortening engine

1. Marie writes warmly.
2. Player misses the tone.
3. Marie becomes short.
4. Player must notice before she stops explaining.

### Photo-test engine

1. Marie sends a photo.
2. The obvious subject is not the real subject.
3. Player must notice the emotional detail.
4. Good answer warms her; bad answer pushes her toward social proof / provocation.

### Nico-revealer engine

1. Nico notices Marie in a social context.
2. Marie reports it as fact, not confession.
3. Player feels jealousy / shame / desire.
4. Marie asks whether he is reacting because he sees her, or because someone else did first.

## 13. J1 usage

For J1, Marie should be the living anchor.

Do not start her as only hurt or suspicious.

J1 Marie should show:

- daily couple energy;
- warmth;
- practical life;
- teasing;
- desire to include Player;
- a first small sign that she notices his absence;
- Mathilde only indirectly if needed;
- no heavy route lock;
- no caricature jealousy.

Good J1 anchors:

- dinner / groceries;
- small outing proposal;
- couch evening that she wants to keep alive;
- dress or hair detail to be noticed;
- message about Mathilde in passing;
- photo that asks for presence, not drama.

Example J1 tone:

```text
Marie : On a des tomates, du fromage, et aucun plan cohérent.
Marie : Donc je propose qu'on appelle ça un dîner.

Player : il manque du pain

Marie : Exact.
Marie : Voilà pourquoi tu es utile quand tu participes.
```

Later, if Player is absent:

```text
Marie : Tu es là ou tu fais décor réaliste ?
```

## 14. Anti-patterns

Avoid making Marie:

- only jealous;
- only suspicious;
- only a moral obstacle;
- a mother who manages Player;
- a victim who waits;
- a woman who forgives because Player says one good line;
- someone who uses Nico only to punish;
- someone whose photos are just sexy decoration.

Avoid repeating too often:

- `pose ton téléphone`;
- `tu ne me regardes pas`;
- `Nico m'a vue avant toi`;
- `tu fais ton ermite`;
- `message reçu`.

These lines are strong, so they need function and spacing.

---

## 15. Final rule

Marie is the living center of the couple.

She is not only the woman Player may lose.

She is:

```text
the dinner that could still be funny,
the walk that could still repair something,
the dress he should have noticed,
the friend table where she becomes bright again,
the photo that asks him to be present,
the partner who refuses to carry the whole mood alone.
```

For any Marie scene, choose one concrete anchor:

- domestic life;
- outing;
- work / event;
- friend group;
- photo;
- Nico as revealer;
- Mathilde / Pauline as social-family context;
- couple repair.

Then ask:

```text
Is Player living this with her,
or only watching Marie become alive somewhere else?
```
