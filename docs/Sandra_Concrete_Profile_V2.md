# Sandra — Concrete Profile V2

> Documentation-only writing addendum.  
> Complements `docs/Character_Playable_Voice_Cards.md`, `docs/Character_Voice_Color_Addendum.md`, `docs/Sandra_Player_Address_Formula_Addendum.md`, and `docs/Character_Voice_Post_Etalons_Addendum.md`.  
> Purpose: make Sandra scenarizable through concrete work, couple, family, hobbies, readings, and trace-based scene engines.  
> No runtime, narrative JSON, tests, assets, or playable content are changed.

## 1. Core diagnosis

Sandra was emotionally coherent but still too abstract.

The earlier profile gave her:

- softness;
- caution;
- nature;
- light;
- photos;
- `haha`;
- `mon cher Player` / `ma chère Sandra`;
- distance-based emotional tension.

But it did not provide enough concrete daily material to generate varied scenes.

This V2 locks her concrete base.

```text
Sandra is not only the woman of soft light.
Sandra is the woman of discreet traces.
```

She does not usually send a frontal emotional confession.

She sends a trace:

- a shift ending;
- a strange incident ticket;
- a printed photo;
- a scrapbook page;
- a book she read too late;
- a weekly visit to her parents;
- a recurring family holiday;
- a small change in the house;
- a silence after a cold argument;
- a detail that looks ordinary but asks to be read.

Player becomes dangerous because he understands that the detail is not just a detail.

## 2. Work

Sandra works on a platform that manages software incidents.

She is first-line support when the software does not work properly.

Her work involves:

- incident tickets;
- user reports;
- priorities;
- procedures;
- repetitive messages;
- bugs;
- handovers between shifts;
- quiet periods waiting for a problem;
- sudden incidents that break the routine;
- being useful without being visible.

Her job is not passionate, but it suits her.

It is concrete, structured, useful, and not too socially exposed.

### Work voice examples

```text

Sandra : Petite victoire sans gloire.
```

```text

Sandra : Je suis restée très polie.

```

```text

Sandra : Ce qui veut dire que j'ai attendu une panne en buvant un café tiède.

```

```text

Sandra : Le logiciel était plus réveillé que moi.

```

## 3. Shift work / 3x8

Sandra works rotating shifts.

This is central to her life.

She can work:

- early morning;
- late evening;
- nights;
- weekends;
- with irregular rest days;
- while her boyfriend sleeps;
- while other people are going out;
- when Player does not expect her to be awake.

This creates a specific kind of intimacy: Sandra can write from empty hours, between shifts, at the edge of normal social time.

### Shift examples

```text
Sandra : Donc mon dîner sera probablement quelque chose de peu glorieux devant une fenêtre noire.

```


## 4. Current couple

Sandra lives in a house with her boyfriend.

The couple is stable, but emotionally underfed.

They are not in permanent war. They have learned to keep the relationship functional by avoiding too much friction.

Their shifted schedules mean they do not always see each other much.

The problem is ordinary but deep:

- he does not always understand her;
- he minimizes what she feels;
- he can find her childish;
- he can call her a crybaby or act as if she is overreacting;
- when they argue, Sandra feels diminished;
- she closes down;
- he often ignores her withdrawal because he sees it as sulking;
- they have learned to tame conflict by ignoring each other.

Sandra still finds balance in the stable life she has.

That stability reassures her, but it can cost her.

### Couple examples

```text
Sandra : On s'est croisés dix minutes ce matin.
Sandra : Il partait, je rentrais.
Sandra : Couple en relais, presque sportif.
```

```text
Sandra : On ne s'est pas vraiment disputés.
Sandra : On a juste échangé trois phrases froides et deux portes fermées.
Sandra : C'est notre version adulte.
```

```text
Sandra : Il dit que je dramatise.
Sandra : Peut-être.
Sandra : Mais c'est fatigant d'être toujours celle qui dramatise quand quelque chose fait mal.
```

```text
Sandra : Je crois qu'on a appris à ne pas se comprendre trop bruyamment.
```

## 5. Conflict response

When Sandra is hurt, she withdraws.

She can:

- stay silent for several days;
- answer very little;
- return to manual activities;
- read;
- do scrapbooking;
- decorate;
- rearrange part of the house;
- visit her parents;
- pretend that things are fine again without really talking.

This is not weakness.

It is a quiet survival method.

Her boyfriend often sees this withdrawal as childish sulking, which makes the silence even colder.

### Withdrawal examples

```text
Sandra : Je n'ai pas beaucoup parlé depuis deux jours.
Sandra : Il a appelé ça "faire la tête".
Sandra : C'est plus simple comme version.
```

```text
Sandra : J'ai rangé toute une étagère au lieu de répondre.
Sandra : Très efficace pour les objets.
Sandra : Moins pour les gens.
```

```text
Sandra : J'ai refait une page de scrap hier soir.
Sandra : Je crois que je colle mieux les photos que les conversations.
```

She needs to feel loved.

## 9. Readings

Sandra reads romantic and emotionally intense fiction.

She can read:

- impossible love stories;
- teenage romance novels;
- dramatic romance;
- complicated or forbidden desire stories;
- books in the broad spirit of `Fifty Shades`-type romance, without copying or pastiching any specific work;
- books she may consider not serious, but that make her feel something.

This is not a noble literary identity.

It is an emotional refuge.

### What the readings reveal

Sandra is reasonable in daily life, but her imagination is less reasonable.

These books reveal:

- her need to be chosen;
- her attraction to stories where someone dares;
- her attraction to trouble contained by fiction;
- her desire to be desired differently;
- her frustration with a flat emotional life;
- her ability to fantasize much more intensely than she speaks.

### Reading examples

```text
Sandra : J'ai lu un truc absolument pas raisonnable avant de dormir.
Sandra : Très mauvaise idée avec mon imagination.
```

```text
Sandra : C'est un roman idiot.
Sandra : Enfin idiot comme "je l'ai fini en deux soirs".
```

```text
Sandra : J'ai lu un roman beaucoup trop dramatique hier soir.
Sandra : Des gens se regardent trois pages et personne ne dort.
Sandra : Ridicule.
Sandra : Évidemment j'ai continué.
```

```text
Player : Tu lis quoi ?

Sandra : Rien de sérieux.

Player : Donc intéressant.

Sandra : Mauvaise conclusion.
Sandra : Exacte, mais mauvaise.
```

### Reading guardrail

Sandra does not confuse life with a novel.

She can even mock herself:

```text
Sandra : Je sais que la vraie vie n'est pas un roman.
Sandra : Déjà parce que personne ne fait la vaisselle dans les romans.
Sandra : C'est très suspect.
```

## 10. New definition of light

The earlier `light` concept was too vague.

In Sandra V2, light must become a concrete trace.

Light can be:

- a failed printed photo;
- low sun on her parents' kitchen table;
- a terrace after a night shift;
- the kitchen window when she returns from work at an odd hour;
- a recurring family holiday photo;
- a reflection on a scrapbook page;
- a soft light that gives her an excuse to send a photo;
- a detail she chooses to keep because it reminds her that she exists quietly.

Rule:

```text
Sandra does not send `light`.
She sends a trace and pretends light is the subject.
```

Example:

```text
Sandra : Officiellement, c'est la lumière sur la table.
Sandra : Officieusement, j'ai laissé mon bras dans le cadre.
Sandra : Accident très discutable.
```

## 11. Scene engine

The core Sandra scene engine is now:

```text
Sandra does not launch a frontal scene.
She sends a trace.
Player must understand what she hid inside the trace.
```

Trace possibilities:

- incident ticket;
- end of shift;
- coffee after night work;
- scrapbook page;
- romance novel read too late;
- old family holiday photo;
- weekly parent visit;
- small house decoration change;
- cold argument minimized;
- silence after being called childish or dramatic;
- defensible blurry photo.

### Typical structure

1. Sandra sends a concrete detail.
2. Player answers with humor or observation.
3. Sandra minimizes.
4. Player sees too accurately.
5. Sandra answers `haha` or dodges.
6. The trace becomes intimate.
7. Sandra sets a small boundary or pulls back.
8. Player respects it, which increases trust.

Sandra is now scenarizable because she has:

- a house;

- a clear communication wound;
- a withdrawal pattern;

## 14. Remaining points to clarify later

Sandra is now much more concrete, but a few details could still improve future scenes.

### 1. Boyfriend profile

Needed later:

- name or placeholder;
- job or rhythm;
- how long they have been together;
- whether he is cold, sarcastic, tired, insecure, or just inattentive;
- one or two recurring phrases he says that hurt Sandra;
- whether he knows Sandra reads romance / impossible-love fiction.

### 2. Family holiday place

Needed later:

- sea / countryside / mountains / village / campsite / family house;
- what object or place returns every year;
- one family ritual;
- what Sandra photographs there.

### 3. House details

Needed later:

- which room is Sandra's refuge;
- where she reads;
- where she does scrapbooking;
- one object she changes or decorates when sad;
- whether the house feels shared or more like two parallel zones.

### 4. Work details

Needed later:

- name/type of platform;
- one colleague;
- one recurring absurd incident;
- tone of work chat;
- whether shifts isolate her socially or give her breathing room.

### 5. Romantic reading style

Needed later:

- does she hide these books;
- does she read digital or paper;
- does she annotate / keep quotes / fold pages;
- what exact emotional pattern attracts her: forbidden love, chosen woman, domination/control fantasy, rescue, slow burn, confession, impossible return?

### 6. Social behavior

Needed later:

- what she looks like in groups;
- how she changes when in confidence;
- who noticed that difference before Player;
- what Player remembers from their earlier friendship.

These points are not blockers for J1, but they will help deeper Sandra routes.

## 15. Final rule Sandra V2

Sandra is the woman of discreet traces.

She does not say directly:

```text
I feel alone.
I need to be loved.
I want you to see me.
```

She writes:

```text
J'ai fini mon shift.
J'ai imprimé une photo floue.
Je passe chez mes parents dimanche.
J'ai changé un cadre dans le salon.
On s'est croisés dix minutes ce matin.
J'ai lu un roman pas très raisonnable.
```

And if Player looks carefully, he understands that these are not details.

They are disguised requests for attention.
