# Character Voice — Post-Etalons Addendum

> Documentation-only writing addendum.  
> Complements `docs/Character_Playable_Voice_Cards.md`, `docs/Character_Voice_Color_Addendum.md`, and `docs/Sandra_Player_Address_Formula_Addendum.md`.  
> Captures the corrections and guardrails validated after writing the first benchmark scenes.  
> No runtime, narrative JSON, tests, assets, or playable content are changed.

## 1. Purpose

After the first benchmark scenes, several risks became visible:

- strong lines can migrate from one character to another;
- some scenes become too rhythmic, too clean, or too predictable;
- a character can accidentally use every one of their signature tools in the same exchange;
- secondary themes are needed so characters do not exist only through desire, jealousy, or route pressure.

This document locks the corrective layer.

```text
A good voice is not a list of catchphrases.
A good voice stays recognizable even when the scene is ordinary.
```

---

## 2. Anti-pattern pass after every benchmark or playable scene

Before validating a scene, run an explicit anti-pattern pass.

Check:

1. Are any strong phrases repeated across several characters?
2. Does a line belong to the character speaking it, or does it sound like the author?
3. Is the scene using too many signature tools at once?
4. Are the replies too perfectly paced?
5. Are there enough banal, short, imperfect replies?
6. Does Player remain active without becoming too brilliant?
7. Is the emotional or flirtatious progression still gradual?
8. Does the scene avoid using all codes of a character in one exchange?

### Shared lines to monitor

These formulas must be monitored because they appeared across several benchmark scenes:

- `trop tard`;
- `question dangereuse`;
- `tu réponds trop bien`;
- `je vais faire semblant`;
- `officiellement / officieusement`;
- `mauvais garçon`;
- `voilà, c'est le problème`.

They are not forbidden, but they must not become author tics.

### Replacement principle

If a formula appears too often, replace it with the character's own grammar.

Examples:

- Sandra: `Je l'ai déjà lu`, `C'est déjà resté`, `Ne me dis pas ça trop bien`.
- Mathilde: `C'est déjà versé au dossier`, `Je vais nier. Mal.`, `Question orientée`.
- Pauline: `Déjà vu`, `Mauvaise nouvelle : j'ai lu`, `Catastrophique`, `mdr non`.
- Raphaëlle: `Je vais laisser cette phrase respirer`, `Je préfère savoir pourquoi tu la poses`.
- Nico: `Mauvaise porte`, `T'assumes mal`, `Fais pas le moine`.
- Marie: `Message reçu. Maintenant message à prouver`, `Ne me réponds pas une phrase molle`.
- Player: `j'ai envoyé trop vite`, `je recommence`, `pas terrible comme réponse`.

## 3. Scene rhythm guardrail

A scene can be good on paper but too automatic in rhythm.

Avoid predictable ladders such as:

```text
photo -> trap -> official/unofficial -> moral joke -> signature code -> confession
```

or:

```text
question -> witty answer -> counter-witty answer -> perfect vulnerable line
```

A more natural Messenger rhythm should include:

- pauses;
- banal replies;
- slightly flat answers;
- topic shifts;
- imperfect jokes;
- a character answering beside the point;
- a dangerous line followed by something ordinary;
- a character returning later to a point they avoided.

```text
The scene must feel improvised by the characters, not executed from the character sheet.
```

## 4. Pauline rhythm correction

Pauline V2 is validated as:

- false sage;
- dry humor;
- public loyalty;
- fast complicity;
- punchlines;
- church choir / parish background;
- no playable voice message.

But her rhythm must not become automatic.

### Dosage

In a Pauline scene:

- `official / unofficial`: maximum once;
- `sage / halo / moral reset`: one or two light touches maximum;
- church choir: one appearance, preferably as a detour or exit beat;
- punchlines: present, but not every reply;
- Marie: background pressure, not a reminder every ten lines;
- Player: sometimes banal, not always perfectly sharp.

### Correct direction

Pauline should not explain her complexity.

She should create complicity in three messages, then realize she is waiting for Player's reply.

Example rhythm:

```text
Pauline : Marie m'a demandé de te l'envoyer.
Pauline : Je précise avant que tu m'accuses de prendre des initiatives.

Player : tu prends des initiatives ?

Pauline : Toujours.
Pauline : Mais là j'ai un témoin.
```

### Choir / no-vocal guardrail

Pauline can talk about singing, rehearsal, or her voice, but the game must never create a playable audio promise.

```text
No voice message is actually sent, played, displayed, or required.
```

Good use:

```text
Player : Tu pourrais m'envoyer un vocal.
Pauline : Non.
Pauline : Ma voix est sous protection paroissiale.
```

Bad use:

- `[vocal sent]`;
- `listen to this`;
- a choice around an absent audio file;
- a scene that requires hearing Pauline's voice.

## 5. Raphaëlle corrections

Raphaëlle is validated as:

- office precision;
- elegance;
- glasses / tied hair;
- calm that is not absence of desire;
- cultures / travel / fabrics / costumes;
- gentle Buddhist-colored attention;
- cats / pandas as rare tender weakness.

### Cats / pandas are rare tools

Do not use cats or pandas systematically.

They are allowed only when they have a clear function:

- soften a hard exchange;
- show that Player knows a tender entry point;
- create a vulnerable smile;
- allow Player to be gentle before giving a real answer.

Normal Raphaëlle scene: no cat / panda.

Tender-fault scene: one cat or one panda, not both.

Seduction scene: possible, but only if followed by a clear sentence.

```text
Cat / panda is an instrument, not a signature obligation.
```

### Travel / culture / Buddhist-colored world

Raphaëlle needs conversation material outside the main tension.

She can talk about:

- travel;
- temples;
- gardens;
- markets;
- tea;
- fabrics;
- motifs;
- broderies;
- notebooks;
- cuisine;
- gestures;
- rituals;
- colors;
- masks;
- costumes;
- accessories;
- Buddhist culture in a broad, gentle sense;
- breathing;
- attention;
- impermanence;
- non-judgment;
- respect for desire.

Guardrail:

```text
Raphaëlle does not give cultural lectures.
She speaks through details, memories, sensations, objects, and gestures.
```

Good tone:

```text
Raphaëlle : J'aime bien les temples où personne ne parle fort.
Raphaëlle : Pas parce que c'est sacré au sens strict.
Raphaëlle : Parce que tout le monde a l'air de faire attention à ses gestes.
```

Bad tone:

```text
Raphaëlle : Dans la tradition bouddhiste, le concept de...
```

## 6. Mathilde corrections

Mathilde is validated as:

- lively;
- social;
- dressed-up;
- slightly chaotic;
- cousin of Marie;
- legal-work background;
- image / outfit / bad faith;
- small domestic catastrophes.

### Legal vocabulary dosage

The legal vocabulary is an accent, not the whole voice.

Recommended dosage:

- normal Mathilde scene: 0 to 1 legal reference;
- light / social scene: 1 maximum;
- work-centered scene: 2 to 3 possible;
- complicity scene with Player: 1 or 2 if Player enters the game.

Avoid concentrated legal sketches such as:

```text
Objection.
Vice de procédure.
Pièce à conviction.
Je plaide non coupable.
Le tribunal appréciera.
```

### Non-legal Mathilde tools

Use more:

- `J'ai rien fait. Enfin presque.`
- `Je vais nier. Mal.`
- `Je voulais faire simple. Raté.`
- `Très moi.`
- `Je suis fatigante.`
- `C'est trop ? Réponds franchement. Non, pas si franchement.`
- `Marie va dire que j'exagère.`

Guardrail:

```text
Mathilde is not funny because she talks law all the time.
She is funny because she sometimes turns embarrassment into a file.
```

### Secondary themes

Mathilde also needs ordinary life material:

- workplace mails;
- colleagues;
- family anecdotes with Marie;
- childhood photos;
- drinks with friends;
- late returns;
- outfit hesitations;
- keys forgotten inside;
- coffee spilled on a shirt;
- umbrella failures;
- public transport mistakes.

## 7. Nico secondary palette

Nico is validated as a more direct and cruder male voice with guardrails.

He reveals Player's more physical and excited male reaction, not only jealousy.

But Nico must not exist only as a sexual / rivalry trigger.

### Recommended environment

Nico should live in a social environment:

- bar;
- light restaurant work;
- event planning;
- venue management;
- night / group plans;
- social network of outings.

This explains why he reads rooms well.

Reference line:

```text
Nico : Je passe ma vie à voir les gens entrer quelque part en faisant semblant de ne pas vouloir être vus.
Nico : C'est une école.
```

### Secondary themes

Nico can talk about:

- bar anecdotes;
- strange customers;
- bad cocktails;
- terraces;
- group plans;
- sport failures;
- darts / pool;
- cold pizza;
- bad brunch decisions;
- blunt but loyal advice;
- social observations.

He can enter a scene without setting fire to it.

```text
Even when Nico talks about a mojito, a bad match, or a failed run,
he observes too well, says things too frankly, and turns almost everything into a game.
```

## 8. Marie secondary palette

Marie must be the living center, not only the dramatic center.

She must give Player a life to return to, not only a relationship to repair.

### Secondary themes

Marie can talk about:

- drinks;
- restaurants;
- terraces;
- invitations;
- friends;
- casual walks;
- groceries;
- tomatoes / bread / burrata;
- laundry;
- couch / series;
- small domestic rituals;
- weather annoyance;
- outfits without crisis;
- hair / lipstick / perfume;
- social plans with Pauline, Mathilde, Nico;
- small funny anger after a bad day.

Reference guardrail:

```text
Marie must make Player want to live with her, not only fear losing her.
```

Good tone:

```text
Marie : On a encore acheté des tomates comme si on était des gens qui mangent des tomates.
Marie : Mensonge annuel.
```

## 9. Player secondary palette

Player must not exist only as a mirror for other characters.

He needs habits, flaws, small wins, traces, and reasons to write first.

### Secondary themes

Player can have:

- office work;
- meetings;
- bad emails;
- cold coffee;
- poor organization;
- forgotten lists;
- too many browser tabs;
- bars / terraces;
- series watched badly;
- books bought faster than read;
- quiet walks;
- simple cooking;
- small photos sent as ordinary traces.

### Occasional chess player

Player can be an occasional chess player.

This fits his profile:

- observant;
- anticipates too much;
- sometimes calculates instead of acting;
- sees patterns;
- looks for the right move;
- misses simple human moves.

Guardrail:

```text
Player is not a chess genius.
Chess colors him; it does not define him.
```

Usable lines:

- `J'ai perdu une partie gagnante. Très moi.`
- `Je réfléchis trop pour un type qui finit quand même par jouer le mauvais coup.`
- `Je suis fort pour anticiper aux échecs. Beaucoup moins en couple.`
- `Je crois que j'attends toujours le bon coup. Mauvaise méthode.`
- `Je suis en train de calculer au lieu de parler. Très mauvais signe.`

Reference line:

```text
Player : Je suis fort pour réfléchir trois coups à l'avance.
Player : Beaucoup moins pour dire la phrase simple au bon moment.
```

The following benchmark directions are validated as calibration material:

- Marie / Player: living couple, sortie, robe, Player returning to presence;
- Sandra / Player: nature / photo / `haha` / address formula, soft distance;
- Pauline / Player: fast complicity, false innocence, rhythm correction needed to avoid pattern automation;

- Mathilde / Player V2: domestic embarrassment, controlled legal humor, Marie in subtext;

These scenes are not final runtime content by themselves.

They are reference calibration for future playable writing.

## 11. Final validation checklist

Before validating future scenes:

- Does each character remain identifiable without the name label?
- Did the scene avoid using every signature tool of the character?
- Are shared phrases replaced by character-specific equivalents?
- Does Player have a real response, even if imperfect?
- Are secondary themes present when the scene is supposed to breathe?
- Are photos or traces functional, not decorative?
- Does the scene avoid turning desire into the only topic?
- Does Marie remain central to the couple's emotional logic?
- Does Nico remain crude and lucid, not vulgar or predatory?
- Does Raphaëlle remain calm and desirable, not a therapist?
- Does Pauline stay quick and lively, not a moral monologue?
- Does Mathilde use legal language sparingly?
- Is Player defined enough to live, but open enough to be played?

```text
If a scene sounds too clean, remove 20% of the cleverness.
If a character sounds too complete, leave one ordinary message in.
If a line could be said by anyone, rewrite it or cut it.
```
