## 1. Purpose

Player must not be a blank avatar.

He also must not become so specific that the user cannot inhabit him.

This profile locks enough concrete material to write him consistently:

- age range;
- work rhythm;
- apartment life with Marie;
- habits;
- flaws;
- humor;
- avoidance patterns;
- occasional chess trait;
- relationship with Marie;
- why Sandra can reach him differently;
- what he must not become.

```text
Player is defined enough to live,
but open enough to be played.
```

---

## 2. Age and situation

Player is around **32–34**.

He has been with Marie for **five years**.

They have lived together for **three years**.

They are not married.

They do not have children.

He is old enough to have routines, responsibilities, and avoidance habits.

He is still young enough for the relationship to feel recoverable, unstable, and actively chosen.

---

## 3. Work

Player works as a **functional project / product operations coordinator** in a small software or digital-services company.

His work is office-based and practical.

It involves:

- meetings;
- specifications;
- follow-up emails;
- bug reports;
- client feedback;
- planning documents;
- calls that could have been emails;
- vague priorities;
- dashboards;
- too many browser tabs;
- cold coffee;
- small decisions delayed too long.

He is not a brilliant startup hero.

He is competent enough, discreet, observant, slightly tired by coordination work, and good at seeing details that other people miss.

### Work examples

```text
Player : J'ai eu une réunion pour décider de refaire une réunion.
Player : Organisation parfaite de l'impuissance.
```

```text
Player : Mon café est froid.
Player : Comme mon envie de répondre au troisième mail de relance.
```

```text
Player : J'ai passé vingt minutes sur un tableau de suivi.
Player : Il ne suit rien.
Player : Mais il est très aligné.
```

## 4. Daily life with Marie

Player lives with Marie in their apartment.

Concrete shared anchors:

- couch;
- open kitchen;
- entrance mirror where Marie gets ready;
- cluttered table;
- plants they forget to water;
- series started and abandoned;
- groceries done badly;
- tomatoes bought too optimistically;
- missing bread;
- evening walks Marie proposes;
- Player's laptop or phone near him too often.

Player is not absent because he does not care.

He is absent because he lets fatigue, habit, and avoidance make him passive.

### Domestic examples


```text
Marie : Ce soir canapé vivant.

Player : je peux être semi-vivant ?
```

## 6. Occasional chess player

Player plays chess occasionally.

This is not a genius trait.

It is a metaphorical color:

- he observes;
- he calculates;
- he sees patterns;
- he sometimes waits too long for the right move;
- he can miss the obvious human move;
- he confuses thinking with acting.

### Chess examples

```text
Player : J'ai perdu une partie gagnante.
Player : Très moi.
```

```text
Player : Je réfléchis trop pour un type qui finit quand même par jouer le mauvais coup.
```

```text
Player : Je suis fort pour réfléchir trois coups à l'avance.
Player : Beaucoup moins pour dire la phrase simple au bon moment.
```

### Guardrail

Do not make Player a chess archetype.

Chess should appear occasionally, as a small self-aware motif.

He uses it when he is:

- embarrassed;
- late;
- caught;
- moved;
- jealous;

- trying not to sound too intense.

His replies are often short.

He can fail and restart.

## 8. Avoidance pattern

Player avoids conflict by becoming vague.

He can:

- answer too late;
- make a joke instead of saying the direct thing;
- overthink;
- stay on the phone;
- observe instead of participating;
- wait for the moment to pass;
- tell himself he is being careful when he is actually avoiding.

His flaw is not cruelty.

His flaw is passive delay.

### Avoidance examples

```text
Marie : Tu es avec moi ou juste dans la pièce ?

Player : mauvaise question
Player : parce que la réponse est probablement humiliante
```

```text
Player : Je pensais répondre mieux plus tard.
Player : Très mauvaise stratégie pour une conversation actuelle.
```

```text
Player : J'ai attendu d'avoir la bonne phrase.
Player : Donc j'ai surtout attendu.
```

## 9. Relationship with Marie

Player loves Marie.

He is not trying to escape her at the start.

But he has started treating the couple as stable enough to require less active attention.

This is the danger.

Marie feels acquired.

Player does not always realize that his passivity has emotional consequences.

### What Player must learn with Marie

- love is not only staying;
- noticing late is not enough;
- being physically present is not the same as joining her;
- reassurance does not replace initiative;
- jealousy is not the same as desire;
- he must come toward Marie before someone else reminds him she is visible.

### Marie examples

```text
Marie : Je ne veux pas être aimée comme une habitude.

Player : ok
Player : là j'aimerais avoir une phrase propre
Player : mais la vraie c'est que je crois que j'ai fait ça
```

```text
Marie : Je veux que tu viennes me chercher.

Player : oui
Player : pas parce que j'ai peur que quelqu'un d'autre le fasse
Player : parce que j'aurais déjà dû le faire
```

## 10. Relationship with Sandra

Sandra reaches Player differently.

Not because she is louder than Marie.

Because she gives him traces to read.

Player is good at noticing details.

Sandra's messages reward that skill:

- a shift ending;
- a paper book on the couch;
- a ghost ticket;
- a photo that pretends to be about light;
- a family house detail;
- a quiet complaint disguised as an anecdote.

This can become dangerous because Player feels useful, precise, and seen as someone who understands.

### Sandra engine for Player

1. Sandra sends a concrete trace.
2. Player notices what is hidden in it.
3. Sandra deflects with `haha` or false reasonableness.
4. Player must decide whether to stay gentle or push too far.

### Sandra examples

```text
Sandra : J'ai fini sur le canapé avec un plaid et un chocolat chaud.

Player : Tu ne fais pas juste un chocolat.
Player : Tu essayes de te remettre dans un endroit où personne ne te parle mal.

Sandra : Haha.
Sandra : Là, tu devrais répondre moins bien.
```

```text
Sandra : Ticket fantôme cette nuit.
Sandra : Quelque chose ne marche pas, mais personne ne sait dire quoi.

Player : donc un ticket très Sandra

Sandra : Très injuste.
Sandra : Pas complètement faux.
```

### Guardrail

Player must not become a perfect reader.

Sometimes he reads too much.

Sometimes he misses.

Sometimes he answers well and then gets scared of what it means.

## 12. Relationship with Pauline, Mathilde, Raphaëlle

### Pauline

Pauline makes Player answer faster and sharper.

She tests his official version.

He must not forget that she is tied to Marie first.

### Mathilde

Mathilde destabilizes Player through proximity, family familiarity, embarrassment, and plausible deniability.

Player must be careful not to treat the harmless setting as harmless by default.

### Raphaëlle

Raphaëlle slows Player down.

She invites clarity, calm, and self-observation.

With her, Player can sound more reflective, but must not become poetic or therapeutic.

## 13. Player scene engines

### Domestic passivity engine

1. Marie sends / creates a domestic moment.
2. Player replies too flat or joins late.
3. Marie tests whether he is really there.
4. Player can repair only if he acts, not just explains.

### Observation engine

1. Another character sends a trace.
2. Player notices a detail.
3. The character reacts to being seen.
4. Player must not overuse precision as seduction.

### Joke-then-truth engine

1. Player jokes because he is embarrassed.
2. The joke is not enough.
3. He corrects himself.
4. The corrected answer becomes intimate.

### Chess-delay engine

1. Player overthinks.
2. He waits for the right line.
3. The moment moves without him.
4. He must either act late or admit he waited too long.

### Jealousy-recognition engine

1. Nico or another external gaze reveals something.
2. Player feels jealousy.
3. He must separate fear of losing from desire to join.
4. Good route: he names it honestly.

## 14. J1/J2 usage

For J1, Player should be:

- present enough that the couple still feels real;
- distracted enough that Marie notices;
- curious enough that Sandra can re-enter naturally;
- not already manipulative;
- not already clear about what he wants;
- sometimes banal;
- sometimes unexpectedly precise.

Strong J1 anchors:

- groceries / missing bread;
- cold coffee;
- work tabs;
- couch evening;
- Marie's mirror / shoes / earrings;
- Sandra old photo / trace;
- Player walking at night;
- a bad but sincere answer.

J2 can increase:

- first jealousy signal;
- more visible Marie social energy;
- first stronger Sandra trace;
- Player trying to answer two emotional worlds with the same avoidance tools.

---

## 15. Anti-patterns

Avoid making Player:

- blank;
- always noble;
- always guilty;
- always clever;
- a perfect seducer;
- a passive camera;
- a man who only says `je ne sais pas`;
- someone who reads every woman perfectly;
- someone who never has ordinary concerns.

Avoid overusing:

- `trop tard`;
- `version honnête / version défendable`;
- `je vais dire un truc con`;
- chess metaphors;
- self-aware guilt;
- perfect observations.

If Player is too clean, make him more ordinary.

If he is too passive, make him act badly or too late.

If he is too smart, make him miss the simple thing.

---

## 16. Final rule

Player is not a blank avatar and not a finished hero.

He is:

```text
a man who notices details,
but not always the right moment;
a man who loves Marie,
but sometimes treats stability as safety;
a man who can read Sandra's traces,
but risks confusing precision with innocence;
a man who jokes before telling the truth,
and sometimes tells the truth too late.
```

For any Player scene, choose one concrete anchor:

- work fatigue;
- domestic passivity;
- cold coffee;
- chess delay;
- Marie's invitation;
- Sandra's trace;
- Nico's bluntness;
- a joke that fails;
- a late correction.

Then ask:

```text
Is Player observing life,
or choosing to enter it?
```
