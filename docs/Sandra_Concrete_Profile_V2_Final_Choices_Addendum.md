## 2. Jeff — final profile

Jeff is **35**.

Sandra and Jeff have been together for **six years**.

They have lived together in their house for **three years**.

Jeff is a mason.

He works early, comes home physically tired, showers, opens his small evening beer, and settles into a simple routine.

He is not a monster.

He is concrete, worn down, not very verbal, and sometimes blunt without realizing how much it hurts.

He loves Sandra in a practical way, but he understands her badly.

He does not know how to join her when she withdraws.

### Recurring Jeff lines

Use sparingly.

- `Tu fais encore ta tête ?`
- `On va pas en faire un roman.`
- `Tu prends tout trop à cœur.`
- `Ça va, je t'ai rien dit.`
- `T'es encore partie dans ton truc.`
- `Franchement, des fois on dirait que tu cherches à pleurer.`
- `Je suis crevé, Sandra. J'ai pas envie de deviner.`
- `Tu peux pas juste dire les choses normalement ?`
- `Bon, tu reviendras quand ça ira mieux.`

### Jeff function

Jeff shows couple erosion rather than immediate villainy.

He gives Sandra:

- a house;
- routine;
- material stability;
- a known life;
- a concrete partner.

He also leaves her with:

- emotional loneliness;
- shame around being sensitive;
- the feeling that her sadness is excessive;
- silence as her safest defense.

Reference line:

```text
Sandra : Jeff ne me fait pas peur.
Sandra : C'est presque le problème.
Sandra : Il me laisse juste devenir silencieuse comme si c'était une météo.
```

## 3. Jeff and Sandra's books

## 4. Family countryside house

The family countryside house is called **la maison des Tilleuls**.

It is in a small countryside village about two hours from Sandra's home.

No specific region is needed for now.

The important feeling is:

- known road;
- pale stone;
- garden;
- old gate;
- wood smell;
- outdoor meals when weather allows;
- predictable rituals;
- family memory.

Sandra has gone there since childhood.

### Concrete details

The house includes:

- an old gate;
- an outdoor table;
- a linden tree in the courtyard;
- a creaking chair;
- a checked tablecloth;
- family photos;
- a bedroom she knows by heart;
- a small path behind the house;
- the Wednesday market;
- the bakery her mother wants to visit early.

### Recurring family ritual

Every summer, the family retakes a photo in front of the old gate.

Everyone says it is ridiculous.

Everyone does it anyway.

Example:

```text
Sandra : Ma mère veut refaire la photo devant le portail cet été.
Sandra : Comme tous les ans.
Sandra : On râle tous.
Sandra : Puis on se place exactement pareil.

Player : Ça te plaît ?

Sandra : Oui.
Sandra : J'aime bien les choses qu'on critique mais qu'on garde.
```

### Narrative function

The house represents what Sandra seeks:

- a place that waits for her;
- stable memory;
- love that does not need to be explained;
- repetition that repairs instead of suffocating.

Reference line:

```text
Sandra : La maison des Tilleuls ne change presque pas.
Sandra : C'est peut-être pour ça que j'aime y retourner.
Sandra : Là-bas, personne ne me demande d'être plus simple.
```

## 5. House with Jeff

Sandra and Jeff's house is practical but emotionally divided.

It includes:

- the living room / couch, Sandra's accessible refuge;
- the bedroom, her stronger withdrawal place;
- a small table or desk corner for scrapbooking;
- frames she changes when she feels low;
- objects Jeff often does not notice;
- the kitchen where Jeff drinks his evening beer;
- a blanket often left on the couch;
- books stacked near the bed or couch.

The house is physically shared but can feel emotionally separated.

### Refuge hierarchy

1. **Couch + blanket + hot chocolate**: Sandra repairs herself gently.
2. **Bedroom + book + closed door**: Sandra withdraws more sharply.
3. **Scrapbooking / frames / decoration**: Sandra restores order in what she can control.

Example:

```text
Sandra : J'ai fini sur le canapé.
Sandra : Plaid, chocolat chaud, livre pas très raisonnable.
Sandra : Programme de survie très digne.

Player : Jeff était là ?

Sandra : Dans la cuisine.
Sandra : Avec sa bière.
Sandra : On était presque dans la même soirée.
```

## 6. SentryCore recurring incident

SentryCore's recurring incident pattern is **the ghost ticket**.

Ghost tickets appear, disappear, then come back without clear logs.

Clients swear that `it does not work`, but screenshots are blurry, times are inconsistent, and nobody has the same error message.

Sandra hates and slightly likes ghost tickets because they resemble her life:

```text
Something is wrong, but nobody manages to document it cleanly.
```

### Ghost ticket example

```text
Sandra : Ticket fantôme cette nuit.
Sandra : Le client dit que le bouton disparaît.
Sandra : Le bouton dit qu'il va très bien.
Sandra : Ambiance couple.

Player : Tu compares ton logiciel à ton couple ?

Sandra : Officiellement non.
Sandra : Officieusement, le bouton communique mieux.
```

### Other possible incident patterns

Use as light work-color details:

- white screen after status change;
- notification sent three times;
- validate button that validates nothing;
- resolved incident that comes back by itself;
- client writing `URGENT` without describing the problem.

Reference line:

```text
Sandra : SentryCore a encore un ticket fantôme.
Sandra : Quelque chose ne marche pas, mais personne ne sait dire quoi.
Sandra : Très reposant comme métaphore.
```

## 7. William — final tone

William is Sandra's colleague on SentryCore.

He is **purely comic / light by default**.

He is not a love interest.

He is slightly childish, never threatening, sometimes annoying, and often useful because he makes Sandra laugh.

He may:

- throw paper balls;
- draw on Post-its;
- make faces during calls;
- invent ticket weather reports;
- make quiet jokes on night shifts;
- leave coffee without ceremony;
- call Sandra `chef de guerre` during a critical incident.

He is not intentionally flirty.

He reveals that Sandra can laugh and relax when someone does not judge her.

### William examples

```text
Sandra : William a fait une météo des tickets.
Sandra : "Averses d'utilisateurs paniqués, risque de mauvaise foi en fin d'après-midi."
Sandra : J'ai ri.
Sandra : Pas professionnel du tout.
```

```text
Sandra : Il m'a lancé une boule de papier pendant que je disais "je comprends votre agacement" au téléphone.
Sandra : J'ai failli mourir professionnellement.
```

```text
Sandra : William a dessiné un fantôme sur le ticket fantôme.
Sandra : Niveau 4 de maturité.
Sandra : Ça m'a fait rire, évidemment.
```

Guardrail:

```text
William is a work color, not a route.
```

## 8. Sandra's exact reading motif

Sandra reads paper romances and assumes it with Player.

What attracts her most:

1. **Impossible love**: two people who should not move closer.
2. **The chosen woman**: someone sees the heroine as nobody else does.
3. **Slow burn**: desire rising slowly, without everything being said.
4. **Delayed confession**: the sentence held back too long.
5. **Control / surrender in fiction**: controlled tension, light domination / surrender fantasy, never a real-life permission by itself.
6. **Contained emotional danger**: feeling strongly without having to destroy real life immediately.

Paper matters: bookmark, cover, book on the couch, folded pages.

She is not ashamed of reading romance with Player.

She may be slightly embarrassed by specific titles or covers.

### Reading example

```text
Player : Encore une histoire impossible ?

Sandra : Oui.
Sandra : J'aime les gens qui savent très bien qu'ils ne devraient pas.
Sandra : Dans les livres, c'est reposant.

Player : Et le côté contrôle ?

Sandra : Haha.
Sandra : Question très indiscrète pour quelqu'un qui n'a pas vu la couverture.

Player : Tu l'as laissée face cachée.

Sandra : Justement.
Sandra : Je suis raisonnable avec la mise en scène.
```

### Reading guardrail

Sandra's books reveal her imagination.

They never give Player a right over her.

Reference line:

```text
Sandra : Mes livres savent beaucoup de choses sur moi.
Sandra : Ça ne veut pas dire qu'ils donnent un mode d'emploi.
```

## 9. Social behavior final

In groups, Sandra withdraws.

She:

- avoids being there if she can;
- speaks little;
- rarely gives her opinion;
- listens;
- observes;
- smiles politely;
- almost disappears behind others;
- remembers many details.

In confidence, Sandra becomes very different.

She:

- talks a lot;
- tells stories;
- complains;
- becomes playful;
- likes gossip;
- leaves an ear out;
- reports juicy anecdotes;
- shows her fragile side;
- becomes more involved and expressive.

### Function

Player must be one of the rare people who sees this Sandra.

The intimacy is not only desire.

It is the passage:

```text
Sandra discreet in groups -> Sandra talkative, vivid, fragile, and playful in trust.
```

### Social example

```text
Player : Tu n'avais presque rien dit à la soirée.

Sandra : Non.
Sandra : Mais j'ai tout entendu.

Player : Tout ?

Sandra : La dispute près de la fenêtre.
Sandra : Le "je m'en fous" de Pauline qui voulait dire exactement l'inverse.
Sandra : Et Nico qui faisait semblant de ne pas regarder Marie.

Player : Tu étais en retrait.

Sandra : Oui.
Sandra : C'est là qu'on entend le mieux.
```

Reference line:

```text
Sandra : En groupe, je disparais un peu.
Sandra : En confiance, je compense.
Sandra : Beaucoup.
Sandra : Tu es prévenu.
```

## 10. What Player remembers from before Marie

Player remembers that Sandra was already present before his relationship with Marie became central.

They were not extremely close, but he had noticed:

- that she spoke little in groups;
- that she sometimes had one very accurate sentence once alone or nearly alone;
- that she laughed more openly when she felt safe;
- that she liked telling afterward what she had observed;
- that she had a secret softness;
- that she seemed more alive in small conversations than in large groups.

### Key first memory

After an old evening, Sandra and Player had a few minutes together while clearing glasses / cleaning up / waiting on a terrace.

She had barely spoken during the evening.

Then, in a small moment, she told him three tiny social dramas she had observed.

Player discovered that she was not absent.

She was attentive.

Example:

```text
Sandra : Tu croyais que je m'ennuyais ce soir-là.

Player : Tu avais dit quatre phrases.

Sandra : Oui.
Sandra : Mais j'avais tout le dossier.

Player : C'est ce soir-là que j'ai compris que tu n'étais pas absente.

Sandra : Voilà.
Sandra : J'étais juste en collecte discrète.
```

### Function

This memory explains why Player can become dangerous for Sandra.

He does not discover Sandra from zero.

He remembers a version of her that few people notice.

## 12. Writing rule

For any Sandra scene, choose **one concrete anchor**:

- SentryCore;
- Jeff;
- blanket / hot chocolate;
- paper romance;
- maison des Tilleuls;
- parents;
- group / gossip;
- photo / scrapbooking.

Then let Player read what the anchor hides.

Do not use all anchors in one scene.

Final rule:

```text
Sandra is quiet in public, trace-based in messages, and emotionally intense in private confidence.
Player matters because he does not only receive her details.
He reads them.
```
