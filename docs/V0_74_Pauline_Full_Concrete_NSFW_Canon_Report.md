## Main canon decisions

- Pauline is 32 and explicitly adult.
- She is Marie's trusted friend of around eight years.
- She works as a retail client adviser in a local mutual bank.
- She lives alone in a rented two-room apartment around fifteen minutes from Marie and Player.
- Her apartment is composed in visible areas but privately imperfect: it is edited, not fake.
- She grew up in a culturally Catholic local community without sexual-repression framing.
- She sings alto in a parish choir, usually on Thursday evenings.
- Choir is a genuine musical and social anchor, not a corruption fetish or permanent joke.
- Pauline is the older of two sisters and learned to be the useful, reliable family member.
- She had a four-year relationship with Bastien, ending around two years before the game.
- Her wound comes from losing authorship of her private self after private fragments were retold by someone she trusted.
- Her deep desire is to be trusted after being seen.
- She is sexually experienced; her route is not a virgin-corruption story.
- Her ordinary style is fitted, controlled, and composed rather than accidentally exhibitionistic.
- Her sensual progression comes from selection: audience, crop, version, timing, and instruction.
- Her adult engine is public trust + selected private channel + controlled image or instruction + reciprocal risk + Player seeing the unscripted reaction + loyalty to Marie.
- Loss of timing, polish, or emotional control never means loss of consent.
- Pauline must become the subject of risk rather than remain a one-way controller of images or proof.
- Marie's friendship is a central route consequence, not background guilt.
- Modular scene pools replace a fixed J4 invitation / J5 photo / later escalation sequence.
- Future runtime keeps three choices by default.
- No playable choir voice message is supported.
