## 3. Exact work: La Verrière

Marie is a **communications and small-events coordinator** at a local cultural / associative venue called **La Verrière**.

La Verrière hosts:

- small exhibitions;
- readings;
- workshops;
- modest concerts;
- association evenings;
- local events;
- vernissages;
- talks;
- informal gatherings.

Marie handles:

- posters;
- social posts;
- newsletters;
- invitations;
- room preparation;
- artist / association coordination;
- calls with city services or suppliers;
- last-minute fixes;
- people who answer too late;
- public smiles while everything is late backstage.

This job fits her because it gives her social movement, visibility, practical stress, and a reason to know many people.

Nico is a social outside presence connected to bars / events / evenings, and possibly to La Verrière's event network.

He reveals what Player stopped noticing, but Marie keeps her own agency.

Sandra is gentler and more distant from Marie.

## 8. Marie's exact wound

Marie's core wound is the fear of becoming **acquired by habit**.

She fears becoming:

- the partner present by default;
- the person who is loved but no longer actively chosen;
- the one Player assumes will still be there;
- the mood-carrier;
- the domestic certainty;
- the woman noticed only when someone else looks.

### What this creates

- humiliation when someone else notices what Player missed;
- fear of becoming banal;
- anger at having to ask for attention;
- sadness at carrying the couple's warmth alone;
- desire to regain power over her own image and evening.

Reference lines:

```text
Marie : Je ne veux pas être aimée comme une habitude.
```

```text
Marie : Le problème, ce n'est pas que tu ne m'aimes plus.
Marie : C'est que parfois tu m'aimes comme si c'était réglé.
```

## 9. Repair mode

When Marie is hurt, she does not withdraw like Sandra.

She moves.

She may:

- put music on;
- tidy quickly and loudly;
- put makeup on;
- change clothes;
- call Pauline;
- go out for a walk;
- accept a drink;
- send or post a photo;
- become bright elsewhere;
- shorten her messages to Player;
- return later with a very clear sentence.

Her repair ritual is:

```text
music + mirror + movement
```

Where Sandra wraps herself in a blanket, Marie puts herself back into motion.

### Repair examples

```text

Marie : Et maintenant je vais marcher.
Marie : C'est ma version calme.
```



## 11. Updated Marie scene engines

### La Verrière engine

1. Marie reports a small event problem.
2. She jokes because she is holding everything together.
3. Player either joins her energy or answers too flat.
4. Marie feels whether he is inside her life or outside watching.

### Domestic warmth engine

1. Marie sends a food / couch / apartment trace.
2. The trace is an invitation to shared life.
3. Player must participate instead of only replying.
4. The ordinary moment becomes a couple test.

### Object / mirror engine

1. Marie mentions a dress, earrings, perfume, shoes, mirror.
2. She does not ask directly to be noticed.
3. Player must notice without being forced.
4. Good answer warms her; late answer hurts her.

### Social brightness engine

1. Marie goes out or prepares to go out.
2. Player hesitates / lags.
3. Marie becomes visible elsewhere.
4. Player reacts, but must decide whether he wants to join her or only fears being replaced.

### Repair movement engine

1. Marie is hurt.
2. She puts music on, moves, changes clothes, walks, calls Pauline.
3. Player has a limited window to join honestly.
4. If he waits too long, she repairs herself outside the couple.

## 13. Anti-patterns

Avoid making Marie:

- only jealous;
- only suspicious;
- only a victim;
- only a moral checkpoint;
- only visible when someone else looks;
- a mother managing Player;
- someone who forgives because Player says one good line;
- someone who uses Nico only to punish.

Avoid overusing:

- `Tu es avec moi ou juste dans la pièce ?`
- `l'ermite`;
- `message reçu`;
- `Nico a remarqué avant toi`;
- direct complaints about Player's phone.

These are strong lines.

Use them only when they change the scene.
